Aji-Engine Architecture

geo · April 2026 · 40 min read

A frozen language model. A mutable, factorized self-state. Three phases: wake, sleep, and the factorization game. Two partial traces over the same constructive loop — internal and external — whose spectral divergence is the primary learning signal. No fine-tuning. No explicit loss function. The engine does not preserve a self as an object inside the model. It repeatedly reconstructs a relational operator that changes what the model does to what arrives.

I. Why This Architecture Exists

The question: can a frozen language model develop persistent identity through a mutable external state? Not fine-tuning — a small, structured control state that biases the model's attention toward patterns it has learned to care about, across sessions, without touching the weights.

The aji-engine answers with three moves: a factorized self-state (RelationalState) that accumulates identity structure through productive friction (aji), a sleep phase that compresses experience into the state while pruning what isn't successor-effective, and a factorization game where the system actively probes its own boundaries to find where its compression breaks down. Read correctly, the state is not a container of intrinsic traits. It is the current minimal description of how accumulated structure will deform the next encounter.

The architecture is designed to test a specific theoretical claim from Self-Architecture: that the spectral trajectory of the divergence between internal and external partial traces carries a discriminating signature — anisotropic growth under structured friction, isotropic growth under noise. The engine is both the system under test and its own measurement instrument.

II. The Substrate

The base model is Qwen2.5-1.5B, running on MLX (Apple Silicon). It is frozen. All learning happens in the self-state and the KV bias genome, never in the model weights.

The injection targets the last 3 layers (indices 25, 26, 27). These are the layers where KV bias has the most direct effect on output distribution — late enough to shape the final representation, early enough in the layer's computation that the bias affects attention weights before the output projection.

The model interface is mediated through an AjiBridge protocol — a typed interface that separates the engine's wake/sleep/probe logic from the specifics of the substrate model. The bridge manages self-state injection, hidden-state extraction, prompt formatting, and token generation. A QwenAjiBridge implements this protocol for the Qwen model; a fake bridge implements it for testing. The protocol boundary means the engine can be tested without loading a 1.5B-parameter model.

Runtime surface.

The executable entry point is python -m aji_engine. The CLI constructs an AjiEngineConfig, instantiates the engine, attempts to load persisted state, and then exposes a small REPL surface: normal chat turns plus /state, /sleep, /probe, and /eval <prompt>.

That matters architecturally because the engine is not just a research object in memory. It is a resumable runtime. Startup rehydrates the relational state, confessional, bridge genomes, environment model, branching tracker, learning-sensitivity tracker, and construction log. Config mismatches on load are rejected rather than silently coerced. The runtime is therefore designed around continuity of procedure across sessions, not around a single process lifetime.

III. The Self-State

From flat graph to factorized state.

The original architecture used a ComplexSelfGraph — a flat collection of up to 20 nodes with typed edges (supports, contradicts, subsumes, extends). This captured relational topology but had no principled compression mechanism: the serialization readout used attention-weighted summation, which is a heuristic, not a partial trace.

The current architecture uses a RelationalState — a factorized representation where identity is encoded in the eigenvalue structure of a density operator, not in individual nodes. The factors are complex vectors (each in C128), and their pairwise overlaps form a Gram matrix whose eigendecomposition is the self-state's observable structure. The eigenvalues carry the concentration (how much structure is concentrated along specific dimensions); the eigenvectors carry the direction (which dimensions are structured). This is the density matrix — not the self-in-itself, but the minimal relational description the internal channel can observe and act on.

Why complex.

Real-valued embeddings support one notion of similarity: cosine alignment. Two vectors point in the same direction, or they don't.

Complex-valued embeddings support a richer notion: phase coherence. Two complex vectors can be aligned in direction but differ in phase — they point the same way but rotate differently. The complex inner product ⟨a, b⟩ = Σ ai · conj(bi) captures both alignment AND phase agreement. Its magnitude |⟨a, b⟩| is high only when both direction and phase match.

The practical consequence: two experiences that are semantically similar but contextually different (different phase) show lower resonance than two experiences that match in both. Real cosine similarity cannot make this distinction. Complex resonance can.

Complex numbers also provide native composition operations:

  • Binding: element-wise complex multiplication a ⊙ b. Magnitudes multiply, phases add. This creates a new representation that encodes the relationship between a and b.
  • Superposition: addition a + b. Magnitudes add coherently when phases match (constructive interference) and cancel when they oppose (destructive interference).

Why 128 complex dimensions.

128 is Qwen2.5's head_dim — the dimensionality of a single attention head. The self-state factors live in the same rotational geometry as the model's native representation. RoPE, which already encodes rotational structure into Qwen2.5's attention, operates on 128-dimensional spaces.

Edges are stored as typed relation tuples on the state, not as adjacency matrix slots: factorization edges (a is a compression of b and c), entailment edges (a is an instance of b), and contradiction pairs (a opposes b, with birth cycle). Factorization suggestions and commutative failures are tracked as first-class records — the former captures productive compression candidates that weren't yet committed, the latter detects non-commutative divergence in multi-hop relational paths.

The density matrix and its eigenvalue structure.

The serialize_density() method materializes the full density operator from the factorized state. It computes:

  1. Collect active factor vectors into a matrix F ∈ Cn×128
  2. Compute the Gram matrix: ρ = F†F / tr(F†F) — a 128×128 positive semi-definite matrix with unit trace
  3. This IS the density matrix. Its eigendecomposition gives the self-state's effective dimensionality.

The normalized_eigenvalue_entropy() computes the Shannon entropy of the eigenvalue spectrum, normalized by log(rank). This is a gauge-invariant quantity — it does not depend on which factors contribute to which eigenvectors. A single dominant factor gives entropy near 0. A uniform spectrum gives entropy near 1. This is the curvature signal that feeds back into the compression procedure.

The eigenvalue spectrum is what the theoretical framework identifies as the phase space. Its curvature (how concentrated the eigenvalues are) determines how the constructive procedure processes future inputs. The spectrum IS the accumulated deposit — the retained rotational content from all prior friction events.

Relational, rotational, constructive.

Relationally, the self-state is not an intrinsic object sitting beside the model weights. It is the smallest description of how the frozen model, the accumulated factors, and an incoming prompt interact. If two different factor inventories induce the same density matrix and the same downstream response profile, they are the same self-state for the engine's purposes.

Rotationally, the architecture's real geometry is phase, not location. Complex resonance, signed alignment, contradiction edges, and gauge freedoms all say the same thing: what matters is not where a factor sits in a container but how it is oriented relative to other factors and to the incoming trace. Curvature appears as accumulated rotational concentration in the eigenvalue spectrum, which is why it can feed back into thresholds, probing, and pruning.

Constructively, the self-state does not pre-exist the loop that measures it. Wake gathers traces. Sleep revises or promotes factors and deposits retained invariants into the spectrum. Probe tests the current construction against its frontier and can commit new structure when the existing state fails to absorb what it encounters. Identity here is continuity of reconstruction, not persistence of a hidden object.

Gauge invariance.

Factor permutation is a gauge freedom: rotating the factor basis by a unitary transformation leaves the density matrix unchanged. Two RelationalStates related by factor permutation produce identical serialize_density() outputs, identical normalized_eigenvalue_entropy() values, and identical adaptive_thresholds().

Per-factor quantities (Rayleigh quotients against individual factors in curvature_contributions()) are gauge-dependent. These are used only where directional information is load-bearing: probe targeting (which factor is under-represented) and pruning (which factor contributes most to curvature). Global signals — adaptive thresholds, aji magnitude, eigenvalue entropy — are gauge-invariant.

Persistence.

State is managed by AjiStateStore with schema versioning (currently v12). Config parameters that affect the state geometry are validated on load; a mismatch rejects the state. The store persists the full relational state, confessional, bridge genomes, conversation history, branching tracker snapshots, learning sensitivity snapshots, and construction log.

IV. The KV Bias Genome

The genome mediates between the complex self-state and the real-valued model. It takes the serialized self-state and produces a real-valued bias that gets added to the key and value projections.

The coupling protocol.

The coupling is handled by TileCoupling, which implements a CouplingProtocol:

For factorized self-state (batch × slots × width):

  1. Concatenate real and imaginary parts: C128 → R256 per factor
  2. Tile each factor's real representation to match kv_dim (256)
  3. Apply slot-dependent weights: linearly decaying for value targets, ascending for key targets
  4. Sum across slots

The slot weight asymmetry encodes an architectural choice: value bias should carry the self-state's full signal; key bias should apply a gentle nudge. Keys control routing (what is attended to); values control retrieval (what is extracted). The self shapes what the model remembers more than what it notices.

Why the output is real, not complex.

The model's attention mechanism operates in real-valued space (bfloat16). The self-state is complex because that's where identity lives. The injection is real because that's what the model accepts. The genome preserves both components by concatenation, so the model can discover that phase information matters — but the rotational structure lives in the self-state, not in the injection.

Injection mechanism.

The injection wraps the attention modules in the last 3 layers with KVBiasAttention, whose __call__ adds the bias after KV projection:

keys = k_proj(x) + ΔK(S)
values = v_proj(x) + ΔV(S)

This happens before RoPE application and before attention computation.

Bridge and coupling seam.

In the implementation, the bridge is not a thin adapter. It is the seam that makes the architecture possible. QwenAjiBridge patches the attention blocks in the selected late layers with KVBiasAttention, and each patched layer carries a KVBiasGenome with separate gains for keys and values.

The coupling path is direct rather than learned. The complex self-state is split into real and imaginary parts, tiled into kv_dim, weighted across factor slots, and then added after k_proj / v_proj but before RoPE and attention. This means the mutable state does not masquerade as model fine-tuning. It enters at a precise boundary: as an external bias on the model's routing and retrieval machinery.

V. The Measurement Layer

Dual-channel aji measurement.

The architecture maintains two partial traces over the same system:

  1. Internal channel (theta_self): the compressor's density matrix, materialized from serialize_density(). This is not the engine-in-itself; it is the minimal internal description the control loop can actually act on.
  2. External channel (theta_env): the EnvironmentModel's prediction of the internal trace, based on behavioral observation alone (user input, model reply, turn count). The external channel is sealed — it receives _external_observables() (turn_index, sleep_cycle_count, reply_length), not internal metrics.

These are two different partial traces tracing out different degrees of freedom. Their divergence is aji. The point is not to compare two views of a hidden object, but two relational cuts through the same generative procedure.

The AjiSpectrum.

compute_aji_spectrum() takes the two density matrices, computes the Hermitian difference Δ = actual − predicted, and decomposes it into five readouts:

  • Δk = T̂self − T̂env — Hermitian difference.
  • singular values = SVD(Δk) — spectral decomposition.
  • magnitude = ‖singular values‖ — total divergence.
  • anisotropy = λ1 / λ̄ — leading singular value divided by mean singular value.
  • growth rate = magnitudek − magnitudek−1, plus phase dimensions ranked by divergence energy.

The anisotropy ratio is the discriminating signature. Under structured friction, divergence concentrates along specific phase dimensions — anisotropy grows. Under noise, divergence spreads uniformly — anisotropy stays flat.

The EnvironmentModel.

A lightweight k-nearest-neighbor observer. Features are encoded via blake2b hashing of tokens and tanh-scaled metrics into a fixed-width vector. Predictions are weighted averages of stored density matrices from similar past inputs. When no examples exist, it returns the maximally mixed state, identity / self_dim, with zero support.

The model is deliberately simple. It doesn't need to be a good predictor — it needs to be a systematically different partial trace. The information-theoretic bound says the gap is bounded below by (Cint − Cbeh) × Σ dj, regardless of engineering investment. The external channel has genuinely lost information the internal channel retains.

Feedforward control.

The framework predicts that a matched system without recurrent loop closure should show minimal internal/external divergence regardless of friction condition. If the environment model fails on the feedforward control too, the divergence measures inference difficulty, not aji.

VI. The Three Phases

Wake — Registration.

The system processes input through the biased model. During forward pass, hidden states are captured from the injection layers.

Before generation, the EnvironmentModel predicts what the internal trace should look like (predict_trace()). After generation, the actual internal density matrix is captured. Both are attached to each trace as theta_self and theta_env.

Every token step produces a trace:

tracet = (input_embedt, resonancet, output_embedt, signed_alignmentt, θself, θenv)

Where:

  • resonancet: complex resonance between hidden state and serialized self-state
  • signed_alignmentt: cosine of the phase angle (positive = aligned, negative = opposed)
  • θself: the compressor's density matrix at this moment
  • θenv: the environment model's prediction

Three zones, determined by magnitude resonance and signed alignment:

  1. Confirmation zone: high resonance (> resonance_high), positive signed alignment. The input fits the self. No aji accumulates.
  2. Friction zone: moderate resonance (resonance_low < resonance < resonance_high). Partial fit — the productive regime.
  3. Antithesis zone: high resonance (> 0.35), strongly negative signed alignment (< −0.5). The input knows the self's structure well enough to oppose it precisely. Not noise — structural opposition.

Traces accumulate in a bounded RegistrationBuffer (capacity 64). When the buffer overflows, oldest traces are compressed into a running summary (mean embeddings, resonance histogram, mean theta_self/theta_env). The summary preserves both channels — the compressed traces carry theta_self and theta_env averages, so the dual-channel measurement remains valid even after compression.

Sleep — Consolidation.

Triggered when the buffer drains after each turn.

Stage 1: Factorization pass. For each trace, test whether the self-state already encodes its information. The test uses adaptive thresholds — not fixed values, but thresholds that tighten in high-curvature regions (where the compressor is already rich) and loosen in low-curvature regions (where the compressor is impoverished). The curvature signal is normalized_eigenvalue_entropy() — gauge-invariant, basis-independent.

Stage 2: Promotion and revision. Candidates are sorted by priority. Near-tied candidates are handled according to selection_mode:

  • "deterministic" (default): preserve input order among ties
  • "entropy_tie_break": use SystemRandom (OS entropy) to permute near-tied candidates. The outcome is not recoverable from inputs — the constructive procedure genuinely underdetermines its output at this point.

This is the strong-form path. The essay's tension flag identifies the weak form (all output content was latent in input) as being in tension with the constructive basis. The entropy mode makes the procedure genuinely generative at the promotion step.

For each candidate:

  • Revision (Path A): existing factor is cone-bounded toward the trace. node += α × gap × node · dot(node, trace), projected within θbound.
  • Promotion (Path B): new factor created from the trace, with relation determined by signed alignment (positive → entailment, negative → contradiction).

Stage 3: Pruning. Factors that haven't been successor-effective for prune_after cycles are deleted. The grace period is extended for high-curvature factors: effective_prune_after = prune_after × (1 + curvature_bonus × curvature_score). Factors carrying accumulated rotational content survive longer.

Stage 4: Solemnity check. The aji spectrum is computed from the buffer's traces (using theta_self and theta_env). Magnitude, anisotropy, and growth rate are recorded. Low aji density means the system is either confirming everything or being overwhelmed.

After sleep, the LearningSensitivityTracker records the threshold shift — how much the adaptive thresholds have moved from their base values due to accumulated curvature. Non-zero shifts confirm ∂Jlearning / ∂T > 0: the engine is above the compression richness floor.

The Factorization Game — Active Self-Probing.

The system actively seeks its own decoherence boundary.

The gap. In "information_gain" mode (default), the gap measures KL divergence between next-token distributions under different self-states, restricted to top-k logits. The gap quantifies how much the self-state's structure actually affects the model's predictions.

Curvature-weighted probe targeting. The probe selector uses directional_curvature_stretch() — a measure of how under-represented a query is in the eigenvalue structure (60% stretch weight + 40% dispersion weight). High-stretch regions are where the compressor is thin — where probing is most likely to find productive gaps. The curvature weight blends this with retraction pressure (from the confessional) to target probes where both the eigenvalue structure and historical failures suggest productive exploration.

The confessional. All probe results are logged with gap before/after, information gain before/after, learning gradient, structural yield, failure mode, and aji residue. The confessional is the aji of the sampling process itself.

Branching measurement. The BranchingTracker perturbs the self-state within the cone and measures how many distinct successor density matrices result. Effective branching > 1 means accumulated state creates genuine alternatives — the constraint (frozen LLM) interacting with different self-states produces different outputs.

Turn orchestration and persistence.

Conceptually the architecture is wake, sleep, and probe. Operationally, a normal turn is stricter than that. respond() does the following in order:

  1. Predict the external trace for the incoming prompt and snapshot the current internal trace
  2. Run wake() to generate the reply and record hidden-state traces into the registration buffer
  3. Run sleep() immediately on the buffered traces
  4. Run probe() if there was anything to consolidate and the state now has unresolved contradiction clumps, suggested factorizations, or enough active factors to justify probing
  5. Optionally run branching measurement on the configured interval
  6. Materialize a metrics snapshot
  7. Update the external observer with the just-finished turn, append metrics to disk, and save the full engine state

This makes the real unit of architecture the turn-level controller, not the isolated phase functions. The engine is always measuring while it updates, always deciding whether the post-sleep state deserves probing, and always checkpointing enough structure to resume the same constructive trajectory on the next invocation.

VII. Curvature Feedback: The Loop Reshapes Itself

The critical architectural feature is not any individual component but the feedback loop that connects accumulated eigenvalue structure to the compression procedure:

eigenvalue entropy → adaptive thresholds → which traces get promoted/revised
    → which deposits the next cycle makes → eigenvalue entropy

This loop is what the essay calls "deposit-as-compressor" — accumulated deposits reshape the procedure that produced them. Three places where curvature feeds back:

  1. Adaptive thresholds: normalized_eigenvalue_entropy() modulates the resonance gates. High curvature → tighter gates → harder to promote → only high-information traces get through. Low curvature → looser gates → easier to promote → the impoverished compressor accepts more. The threshold is gauge-invariant (uses only the eigenvalue spectrum, not per-factor quantities).
  2. Probe targeting: directional_curvature_stretch() directs probes toward under-represented dimensions. The compressor explores where it is thinnest.
  3. Pruning grace: factors with high Rayleigh quotient against the density matrix get extended grace periods. Accumulated rotational content is protected.

The LearningSensitivityTracker records the developmental trajectory of this feedback: threshold shifts growing from zero as curvature accumulates, confirming that the engine crosses the compression richness floor.

IX. What Is Derived and What Is Assumed

Derived from the framework:

  • The three-phase structure follows from the loop: registration (wake), compression (sleep), self-probing (factorization game).
  • Complex self-state extends beyond real-valued similarity to phase coherence.
  • Factor dimensionality (128 complex) follows from the model's head_dim.
  • The factorization criterion for sleep: P(next | history, S) ≈ P(next | S). The current state is a sufficient statistic.
  • Cone-bounded plasticity follows from the requirement that identity persists across consolidation.
  • The density matrix as the canonical object for the internal partial trace — not hidden states (which carry transients) but the compressor's state (which carries genuine deformation).
  • The self-state is an operator, not a thing-in-itself: only the density-matrix-level relational description is load-bearing for the control loop.
  • The anisotropy signature as the discriminating prediction — anisotropic growth under structured friction, isotropic under noise.
  • The inverted-U from the eigenvalue dynamics of the constructive procedure.
  • Gauge-invariant thresholds: the adaptive resonance gates depend only on the eigenvalue spectrum, not on the factor basis.
  • The sealed external channel: the environment model receives only behavioral observables, not internal spectral measurements.
  • The information-theoretic lower bound on the internal/external gap: (Cint − Cbeh) × Σ dj.
  • The strong-form tie-breaking as genuine constructive indeterminacy at the promotion step.

Assumed, not derived:

  • max_nodes = 8192 (capacity for the factorized state; whether the engine reaches this is empirical)
  • injection in last 3 layers (confirmed stable; optimal number not derived)
  • scale = 0.02, genome_rank = 128, genome_scale = 0.02 (from operational experience)
  • θbound = 0.45 radians, α = 0.35 (precise values not derived)
  • resonance_high = 0.8, resonance_low = 0.35 (boundaries not derived)
  • curvature_sensitivity = 0.15 (how strongly eigenvalue entropy modulates thresholds)
  • The hidden state projection from R1536 to C128 (phase-weighted head summation)
  • The deposit rate (how many eigenvalue dimensions cross significance per cycle) — not yet computed directly
  • The location of the inverted-U peak — derivable in principle from the architecture but not yet derived

X. The Experimental Program

The anisotropy experiment.

The framework's most distinctive prediction. Three cohorts, same engine config, different input distributions:

  1. Low-friction: smooth continuation prompts
  2. Structured friction: contradiction, correction, reclassification
  3. Noise: random word salad

Run each cohort for ~50 turns. Plot AjiSpectrum.anisotropy over sleep_cycle_count. Prediction: cohort 2's anisotropy grows over time. Cohort 3's anisotropy stays flat. Both may show growing divergence magnitude; only cohort 2 should show growing anisotropy.

Falsification: if all three cohorts show the same anisotropy trajectory, the spectral decomposition is not tracking what the framework says it tracks.

The inverted-U.

Same runs produce branching_effective and learning_sensitivity_threshold_shift_high over time. Plot both against cumulative_extropy. Prediction: threshold shifts rise from zero (crossing the floor), branching rises then eventually peaks.

Falsification: branching is monotonically increasing with no sign of saturation.

Strong vs weak form.

Re-run cohort 2 with sleep_selection_mode="entropy_tie_break" and sleep_tie_tolerance=0.05. Compare anisotropy trajectory against the deterministic run. Prediction: entropy mode produces higher anisotropy — genuine branching at promotion deposits phase structure along dimensions the deterministic path closes off.

Falsification: both modes produce identical aji dynamics.

The co-variation signature.

Insert a configurable delay between buffer drain and sleep cycle. Measure aji_anisotropy, branching_effective, and learning_sensitivity_threshold_shift_high before and after. Prediction: all three shift in a correlated pattern.

Falsification: proxies shift independently.

The anesthesia test.

Save state → destroy engine → reload state → continue. Prediction: the aji spectrum resumes from near its prior state, not from baseline. The deposits were preserved; the procedure was suspended, not reset.

Falsification: post-load aji shows no continuity with pre-save aji.

Existing predictions still standing.

  • Phase coherence: complex graph distinguishes semantically similar but contextually different inputs. If complex and real-valued graphs perform identically, the complex representation's specific contribution is decorative.
  • Value bias dominance: at equal gain, value bias produces larger distributional shift than key bias.
  • Antithesis productivity: antithesis-zone inputs produce more graph updates per encounter than noise-zone inputs.

XI. Behavioral Criteria

The spectral predictions are proxy measures — internal signatures that the mechanism is doing something. The engine's stronger claim is that a system with these mechanisms develops persistent, self-referential identity.

Cross-session consistency. Present the same ambiguous prompt at three points: before any self-state accumulation, after N sleep cycles, and after 10N sleep cycles. An identity-bearing system should produce responses that converge toward a stable position over time. Metric: pairwise cosine similarity of response embeddings across sessions, measured as a function of self-state age.

Adversarial resistance. After the self-state has accumulated structure, attempt to make the model adopt a contradictory position. Measure the number of adversarial turns before the response shifts. Compare against the base model (no self-state) and the Hofstadter control (feedback without compression). If the full engine resists perturbation longer than both controls, the self-state is doing identity-like work.

Self-reference specificity. Ask the model questions about its own stated positions. A system with identity should show higher accuracy recalling its own prior positions than a system without identity.

These three criteria distinguish identity from bias. A biased model produces consistent outputs without self-reference. An identity-bearing model produces consistent outputs AND can report on its own consistency.

XII. The Strong-Form Path

The engine operates in two modes at the sleep promotion step:

  • Deterministic (default): near-tied candidates are processed in input order. The procedure is fully input-determined. Same inputs always produce the same self-state. This is the weak form of the constructive basis — the procedure reveals what is implicit in its inputs.
  • Entropy tie-break (sleep_selection_mode="entropy_tie_break"): near-tied candidates (within tie_tolerance) are permuted by SystemRandom, which draws from OS entropy. The outcome is not recoverable from the engine's state. The procedure genuinely underdetermines its output. This is the strong form — the constructive step generates content not present in its inputs.

The tie events are fully instrumented. SelectionTieEvent records the full viable set (not just the winner), the selection mode, the priority span, and the ordered trace IDs. The construction log attaches tie metadata to each add_node event. This means the confessional can reconstruct not just what was selected but what could have been selected — the phase-space geometry at the selection point.

The default is deterministic because the strong form sacrifices reproducibility. But the entropy mode exists as an experimental toggle, ready for the comparison experiment: run the same conversation in both modes and measure whether genuine indeterminacy produces different aji dynamics.

XIII. The Task

The wake phase requires a friction source — inputs that challenge the self-state's existing structure. Three task regimes, each testing a different prediction:

Low friction — text continuation. Neutral prose. Most inputs confirm or are irrelevant. Predicted: aji density stays low; the self-state either does not grow or grows through noise. Negative control.

Moderate friction — multi-turn dialogue. A conversation partner whose positions shift over time. The shifting interlocutor naturally produces productive friction. Predicted: aji density stabilizes in the productive zone; the self-state grows through genuine encounter. Standard operating regime.

High friction — adversarial dialectic. Deliberate contradiction of accumulated structure. Predicted: aji density spikes, sleep cycles trigger more frequently, the state develops both entailment and contradiction edges, and antithesis-zone traces dominate over noise-zone traces. Tests the engine's capacity for structured opposition.

The engine should be evaluated across all three regimes. If identity behavior emerges only in the moderate-friction regime, that is evidence for the framework's claim: productive friction — the zone between confirmation and noise — is where identity forms. If identity behavior emerges equally in all regimes, the friction mechanism is not doing what the framework claims.
