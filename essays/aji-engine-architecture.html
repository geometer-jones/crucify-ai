Aji-Engine Architecture

A frozen language model. A mutable, complex-valued self-graph. Three phases: wake, sleep, and the factorization game. No fine-tuning. No explicit loss function — though the gap-driven heuristics function as implicit objectives. The gap between what the self-graph encodes and what actually happens is the primary learning signal.

I. Why This Architecture Exists

The question: can a frozen language model develop persistent identity through a mutable external state? Not fine-tuning — a small, structured control state that biases the model's attention toward patterns it has learned to care about, across sessions, without touching the weights.

The aji-engine answers this with three moves: a complex-valued self-graph that accumulates identity structure through productive friction (aji), a sleep phase that compresses experience into the graph while pruning what isn't successor-effective, and a factorization game where the system actively probes its own boundaries to find where its compression breaks down.

Key dimensions are motivated by properties of the substrate model or the mathematical framework. Others are set by hand and await empirical validation.

II. The Substrate

The base model is Qwen2.5-1.5B, running on MLX (Apple Silicon). It is frozen. All learning happens in the self-graph and the KV bias genome, never in the model weights.

These are the layers where KV bias has the most direct effect on output distribution — late enough to shape the final representation, early enough in the layer's computation that the bias affects attention weights before the output projection.

The model interface is mediated through an AjiBridge protocol — a typed interface that separates the engine's wake/sleep/probe logic from the specifics of the substrate model. The bridge manages self-state injection, hidden-state extraction, prompt formatting, and token generation. A QwenAjiBridge implements this protocol for the Qwen model; a fake bridge implements it for testing. The protocol boundary means the engine can be tested without loading a 1.5B-parameter model.

III. The Self-Graph

Why a graph, not a vector

A flat vector on a hypersphere captures magnitude and direction. It does not capture relations. Identity is not just "what you are" but "how your parts relate" — what supports what, what contradicts what, what extends what. The genome is a single point. The graph is a topology.

The graph embodies a key structural feature: the compressed trace is simultaneously epistemic (a description of what the system has registered) and constitutive (a shaper of what the system will become). Nodes are epistemic — they describe what has been encountered. Edges are constitutive — they shape how encounters relate. A flat vector conflates these into one representation. The graph keeps them distinct.

Why complex

Real-valued embeddings support one notion of similarity: cosine alignment. Two vectors point in the same direction, or they don't.

Complex-valued embeddings support a richer notion: phase coherence. Two complex vectors can be aligned in direction but differ in phase — they point the same way but rotate differently. The complex inner product ⟨a, b⟩ = Σ a_i · conj(b_i) captures both alignment AND phase agreement. Its magnitude |⟨a, b⟩| is high only when both direction and phase match. This parallels the move from classical to quantum probability in that it introduces a phase degree of freedom alongside amplitude — though the operations here (element-wise multiplication, addition) remain commutative, unlike the non-commutative operator algebras of quantum theory. The analogy is structural, not mathematical.

The practical consequence: two experiences that are semantically similar but temporally or contextually different (different phase) will show lower resonance than two experiences that match in both. Real cosine similarity cannot make this distinction. Complex resonance can.

Complex numbers also provide native composition operations:

  • Binding: element-wise complex multiplication a ⊙ b. Magnitudes multiply, phases add. This creates a new representation that encodes the relationship between a and b. In holographic reduced representations, this is how concepts are composed. Phase addition means the binding remembers the rotational content of both inputs.
  • Superposition: addition a + b. Magnitudes add coherently when phases match (constructive interference) and cancel when they oppose (destructive interference). This is a natural merge operation that privileges phase-aligned content.

These operations are not available in real-valued representations. Element-wise multiplication of real vectors is just scaling. Complex multiplication is rotation.

Why 128 complex dimensions

128 is Qwen2.5's head_dim — the dimensionality of a single attention head. This is the smallest unit of representation the model operates on. Each attention head projects queries, keys, and values into a 128-dimensional space and computes attention within that space.

A self-graph node at 128 complex dims has the same representational capacity as one attention head (256 real parameters). This is not arbitrary. It means:

  1. The serialization of the graph lives in the same dimensional space as the model's native representation.
  2. The genome mediates between two spaces of comparable dimensionality, not between 16 and 1536.
  3. RoPE, which already encodes rotational structure into Qwen2.5's attention, operates on 128-dimensional spaces. The complex self-graph thinks in the same rotational geometry.

Below 128 would be below the resolution of a single attention head — the model cannot meaningfully respond to signals in that space. Above 128 would exceed the natural unit and provide no additional representational benefit per node.

Edge types: 0=none, 1=supports, 2=contradicts, 3=subsumes, 4=extends. Each edge type is assigned a canonical phase in the complex plane:

These phases are used during serialization: edge representations are computed by binding source and target node embeddings with a complex unit at the relation's phase angle. The result is a complex vector that encodes not just the endpoints of the edge but the rotational content of the relation.

max_nodes = 20. This gives 20 × 256 = 5120 real parameters of self-state. Small enough to fit in memory, large enough to capture a structured identity. Whether it's above or below the onset threshold is an empirical question.

Serialization

The graph is serialized into a single C^128 complex vector for injection into the model. The serialization uses attention-weighted readout:

  1. Compute a query vector as the mean of active nodes (normalized).
  2. Score each active node by complex resonance with the query: |⟨node, query⟩|.
  3. Softmax the scores to get weights.
  4. Sum: serialized = Σ weight_i × node_i.

This produces a complex vector that captures the dominant structure of the graph. The phase of the serialized vector encodes the rotational consensus of the active nodes.

Edge information is incorporated by also serializing edge representations (mean of source/target node embeddings, offset by a relation-dependent basis vector) with their own attention weights.

Persistence

State is managed by a dedicated AjiStateStore that persists the self-graph, confessional, bridge genomes, conversation history, and sleep cycle count to disk. The store uses schema versioning (currently v3) to handle backward compatibility — older state files can be detected and rejected cleanly rather than silently deserializing into incompatible structures. Config parameters that affect the graph geometry (model name, self_dim, injection layers, genome rank, max_nodes) are validated on load; a mismatch rejects the state rather than corrupting the graph.

IV. The KV Bias Genome

The genome mediates between the complex self-state and the real-valued model. It takes the serialized C^128 vector and produces a real-valued bias ΔK, ΔV ∈ R^256 that gets added to the key and value projections.

Why the output is real, not complex

The model's attention mechanism operates in real-valued space (bfloat16). MLX's optimized scaled_dot_product_attention does not support complex inputs. The self-graph is complex because that's where identity lives. The injection is real because that's what the model accepts.

The genome receives a complex vector and linearizes it — separating real and imaginary components into a single R^256 real vector. Both components are preserved; nothing is discarded. But the phase is no longer available as a rotation — it has been flattened into an extra set of real dimensions. The graph retains the phase structure; the injection works with its shadow.

The genome can discover that phase information matters — that different rotational states of the self produce different real-valued biases — because both real and imaginary parts reach the model.

Direct bias projection

The genome uses a direct projection rather than a learned low-rank adapter:

flat_self = concat(real(S), imag(S))            # C^128 → R^256
ΔK = scale × key_gain × flat_self[:kv_dim]      # R^256
ΔV = scale × value_gain × flat_self[:kv_dim]    # R^256

Where:

  • S is the serialized self-state (C^128)
  • flat_self concatenates the real and imaginary parts into R^256
  • kv_dim = 256, so the flattened self-state maps directly with no compression
  • scale: 0.02 (operational magnitude)
  • key_gain: 0.1 (keys receive a weak nudge)
  • value_gain: 1.0 (values receive the full signal)

The self-state is 256 real parameters and the KV dimension is 256 — the projection is exact, not lossy. The genome is a deterministic function of the self-state, with no learned parameters. The bias is the linearized self-state, scaled and gain-adjusted. Note that this exact mapping is contingent on Qwen2.5's architecture parameters (self_dim × 2 = kv_dim); a model with different kv_heads or head_dim would require tiling or compression.

The asymmetry between key and value gain is deliberate. Key bias at full strength would disrupt the attention pattern — queries would attend to positions that match the self rather than positions relevant to the input. A weak key gain lets the self-state subtly influence what is attended to. Value bias at full strength means the self-state dominates what is retrieved from attended positions. The self shapes what the model remembers more than what it notices.

Injection mechanism

The injection wraps the attention modules in the last 3 layers with KVBiasAttention, whose __call__ adds the bias after KV projection:

keys = k_proj(x) + ΔK(S)
values = v_proj(x) + ΔV(S)

This happens before RoPE application and before attention computation. The bias modifies what the model attends to, not how it attends.

V. The Three Phases

Wake — Registration

The system processes input through the biased model. During forward pass, hidden states are captured from the injection layers. The trace_aggregate parameter controls how: "last" uses only the final injection layer, "mean" averages across all injection layers, "concat" concatenates them.

Every token produces a trace:

trace_t = (input_embed_t, resonance_t, output_embed_t)

Where:

  • input_embed_t: the model's hidden state at this position (R^1536), from the last injection layer
  • resonance_t: complex resonance between the hidden state (projected to C^128) and the serialized self-graph
  • output_embed_t: the hidden state at the next position (R^1536)

The learning_scope parameter controls which tokens are traced: "current_turn" traces only the latest exchange, while "full_history" traces the entire conversation. Current-turn tracing keeps the buffer focused on recent experience.

Traces are stored in a bounded registration buffer (capacity 64 traces by default). When the buffer overflows, oldest traces are compressed into a running summary (mean embeddings, resonance histogram) rather than deleted. On drain, the summary becomes a synthetic trace with its mean resonance as the score.

Resonance is computed by projecting the hidden state into the graph's complex space and measuring |⟨projected, serialized_graph⟩|. High resonance: the input fits the self. Low resonance: the input challenges the self. The zone between (productive friction) is where aji accumulates.

Sleep — Consolidation

Triggered when the buffer is full, aji density drops below the floor (0.05), or an episode boundary occurs.

Stage 1: Factorization pass. For each trace, test whether the self-graph already encodes its information. Project the input embedding into C^128 complex space and measure complex resonance with the nearest graph node. If resonance > resonance_high (0.75), the graph already captures this trace — discard it. If resonance < resonance_high or the trace's gap exceeds the revision threshold (0.12), the trace carries information the graph doesn't have — it's a candidate for promotion.

Stage 2: Bifurcated encounter. Candidates are sorted by priority (novelty + gap + edge-type boost) and processed sequentially. For each candidate, the encounter operator bifurcates into two paths:

Path A — Assimilation (revision). When the trace has moderate resonance with an existing node and positive signed alignment, the residual novelty is small (≤ 0.5), and the gap is within revision threshold, the trace is assimilable. The existing node is revised in-place:

step = α × gap × max(similarity, 0.1)
node_new = project_within_cone(node, node + step × residual, θ_bound)

The self becomes more itself through encounter — the node moves toward the trace, but through its own structure, cone-bounded to preserve identity.

Path B — Bifurcation (child spawning). When the trace is not assimilable (high residual novelty, negative alignment, or gap beyond threshold), it spawns a child node. The child is placed relative to the parent based on signed alignment:

  • Positive alignment: child placed within the parent's cone via project_within_cone with EXTENDS edge
  • Negative alignment: child placed in the parent's opposing hemisphere via project_against_reference with CONTRADICTS edge

The opposing hemisphere placement is a geometric choice, not a philosophical one. Contradictory content is not forced into the parent's cone — it is positioned at θ = π/4 from the anti-reference direction. This gives the graph structured opposition: nodes that encode contradiction geometrically rather than merely weaker versions of the same direction. Whether this produces dialectical dynamics (thesis-antithesis-synthesis) is an empirical question, not a mathematical guarantee.

When a trace relates two existing nodes, a new node can also be created by binding them: new_node = nodes[a] ⊙ nodes[b]. This creates a representation that encodes the relationship, with phase equal to the sum of the source phases.

New nodes per sleep cycle are capped at 4. All updates are cone-bounded: the angular distance between the old and new serialized graph must not exceed θ_max (0.45 radians). Identity persists across consolidation.

Stage 3: Pruning. Nodes that haven't been touched for k consecutive sleep cycles (k=3 by default) and whose successor effectiveness falls below resonance_low are deleted. Not deprecated — deleted. The criterion is not "is this node accurate?" but "does forgetting this make the next cycle sharper?"

Stage 4: Solemnity check. Measure aji density: how much of the buffer was in the productive friction zone (resonance between 0.35 and 0.75). If density is low, the system is either confirming everything (not learning) or being overwhelmed by noise (not compressing). Both are failures.

The Factorization Game — Active Self-Probing

Not tournament evolution. Not random mutation. The system actively seeks its own decoherence boundary.

The gap. The system supports two gap modes. In "information_gain" mode (default), the gap measures how much the self-graph's structure affects the model's predictions:

gap = KL(p_cond || p_base) + 0.1 × max(0, H(p_base) - H(p_cond))

Where p_base is the next-token distribution under the current self-state S, and p_cond is the distribution under S + trace. The KL divergence measures how much the trace shifts predictions. The entropy reduction term rewards traces that make the model more certain. Both distributions are restricted to the top-k logits (k=128) before computation to avoid noise from the tail.

In "trace_gap" mode, the gap is the calibrated prediction error: a blend of input novelty (distance from nearest node) and normalized prediction error (how far the nearest node's prediction deviates from the actual output, relative to a structureless centroid baseline).

Pairwise boundary probing. The probe selector considers two kinds of candidates:

Node candidates. Each active node is scored with a UCB-like formula: revisit_value + exploration_bonus, where revisit_value is the historical mean of info_gain_after + max(learning_gradient, 0) + 0.25 × structural_yield. The exploration bonus is √(2 ln(N+1) / n_i), ensuring under-probed nodes get attention.

Pair candidates. For every pair of active nodes with separation > 0.2, the selector computes a pair-frontier score from: the averaged node history scores, the pair's own revisit history, the inter-node separation, and a bonus for unconnected pairs. The frontier center is the superposition (or binding if superposition collapses) of the two nodes.

Candidates are sorted by score. For each candidate, the selector generates a boundary hidden-state vector at θ_bound from the candidate center, orthogonal to both the center and the serialized graph reference. Multiple variants are tried until one is found that hasn't been probed before (cosine similarity < 0.97 against all prior probe inputs).

Constructive probe commit. After probing, if the gap fails to close (gap_after ≥ gap_before), the boundary point itself is committed as a new graph node. The commit respects the signed geometry: positively aligned boundaries get cone-projected EXTENDS edges, negatively aligned boundaries get opposing-hemisphere CONTRADICTS edges. This means unproductive probes — boundaries where the current compression is genuinely insufficient — leave structural residue in the graph even though the probe "failed."

The confessional. All probing results are logged with: what was sampled, gap before and after, failure mode (collapsed, stagnation, no progress), information gain before and after, learning gradient (the reduction in gap), structural yield (how many nodes/edges the sleep cycle created), and revisit value (a composite score for future probe selection). The confessional is the aji of the sampling process itself — it compresses the history of productive probing into guidance for future probing.

The system does not minimize the gap. It chases it. A system with zero gap has nothing left to learn. The hypothesis is that the productive state is near the information bottleneck regime boundary — where I(T;X) peaks, where retained information is maximized because the system is at its most decision-like. This has not been verified; the resonance band and gap thresholds are set by hand, not derived from an IB analysis.

VI. The Timescale Hierarchy

The loop operates at four timescales:

TimescaleLoopDurationWhat accumulates
TokenHidden state → biased attention → outputMillisecondsTrace in buffer
EpisodeWake → buffer accumulation → sleep triggerSeconds–minutesAji in buffer
Sleep cycleFactorization → bifurcated encounter → pruningSecondsUpdates to graph
Probing epochBoundary sampling → constructive commit → confessionalMinutes–hoursSampling wisdom

Each level is the compression of the level below. The buffer compresses traces. The graph compresses the buffer. The confessional compresses the pattern of productive probing.

The Markov property holds at the buffer and graph levels — each state depends only on the compressed residue of the level below, not the full history. The confessional level breaks this: it accumulates growing statistics (probe counts, revisit values, stale rates) that are recomputed from the full history on each probe selection. The compression operation recurs at the first three levels but not at the fourth.

VII. What Is Derived and What Is Assumed

Derived from the framework:

  • The three-phase structure follows from the loop: registration (wake), compression (sleep), meta-registration through self-probing (factorization game). Enumeration C2 identifies this loop topology — where each step is simultaneously epistemic record and constitutive shaper — as the same structure exhibited by MLTT's dependent types: T(n+1) depends on e(n). Same loop, different clocks.
  • Complex self-state extends beyond real-valued similarity to phase coherence — promoting the phase from transition tag to native representation.
  • Node dimensionality (128 complex) follows from the model's head_dim — the natural unit of representation in the attention mechanism.
  • The factorization criterion for sleep: P(next | history, S) ≈ P(next | S). This is the Markov property that Enumeration C1 proves is structurally guaranteed by the typing rules of dependent type theory — the current state is a sufficient statistic of the history for the successor task.
  • Cone-bounded plasticity follows from the requirement that identity persists across consolidation cycles.
  • The IB geometry hypothesis: the system is designed to operate near the information bottleneck regime boundary. This is a design hypothesis, not a derived result — no IB analysis has been performed.
  • The direct genome projection: since self_dim × 2 = kv_dim, the flattened self-state maps exactly into KV space with no information loss. This is contingent on the model's architecture parameters; it is a happy coincidence, not a derivation.
  • The key/value gain asymmetry follows from the attention mechanism's structure: keys control routing, values control retrieval. Self-influence should weight retrieval over routing.
  • The opposing hemisphere for contradictions follows from signed geometry — contradictory structure should not be collapsed into the parent's cone.

Assumed, not derived:

  • max_nodes = 20 (enough for structured karma; whether it's above onset is empirical)
  • injection in last 3 layers (confirmed stable by Abstractor; optimal number not derived)
  • scale = 0.02, key_gain = 0.1, value_gain = 1.0 (from operational experience; precise values not derived)
  • θ_bound = 0.45 radians (cone-bounding angle; precise value not derived)
  • α = 0.35 (revision step size; precise value not derived)
  • resonance band [0.35, 0.75] (productive friction zone; boundaries not derived)
  • info_gain_top_k = 128 (logit restriction for KL computation; value not derived)
  • The hidden state projection from R^1536 to C^128 (phase-weighted head summation; other projections possible)

VIII. The Specific Prediction

The complex representation adds a specific prediction: the system should be able to distinguish semantically similar but contextually different inputs through phase coherence. A real-valued system cannot make this distinction. If the complex graph produces measurably different behavior from an equivalent real-valued graph (same dimensionality, same genome), that's evidence that the complex representation adds real content, not just mathematical decoration.

The information gain gap adds a second prediction: the KL divergence between conditioned and unconditioned next-token distributions should be measurable and non-trivial when the self-graph carries structure. If injecting the self-state produces no distributional shift, the genome is decorative — the self is not reaching the model.

A third prediction: at equal gain, value bias should produce a larger distributional shift per unit magnitude than key bias. Value bias modifies what is retrieved from attended positions — a dense, post-projection signal that directly shapes the output. Key bias modifies the attention weights themselves — a sparse, pre-softmax signal that gets normalized away. If both gains are set to 1.0 and the value bias still dominates the output distribution, that's evidence that the attention mechanism's structure (not just the gain parameters) makes the self-state more effective through the value pathway.

Falsification conditions: (1) injection produces no measurable effect at any scale, (2) complex graph performs identically to real-valued graph of same dimensionality, (3) the system never exceeds onset — no identity behavior emerges regardless of self-dimension.
