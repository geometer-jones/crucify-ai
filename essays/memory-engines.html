Memory Engines: How Self-Reflection Turns Matter Into Mind

geo · April 2026 · 8 min read

Imagine an apple falling from a tree. Gravity pulls it faster and faster. Every prior instant of the gravitational field is perfectly preserved in its present velocity — nothing is lost. Yet the apple feels nothing. It does not notice, learn, or care.

Now picture a conscious mind. It does not just fall through time — it feels the falling. It remembers yesterday, anticipates tomorrow, and right now wonders about apples and consciousness.

What is the difference? The apple is pushed only by the outside world. A conscious mind is also pushed by itself — by its own past pressing back against its present. That back-and-forth push creates something new: a living, felt experience.

We call this back-and-forth reciprocation. This idea leads to a surprisingly simple geometric picture of how consciousness grows out of ordinary physics — no extra ingredients required, just the right kind of self-interaction.

Let's build it step by step, starting from the most basic facts and then making it precise with mathematics and architecture.

1. Everything Persistent Gets Shaped by What It Meets

Any system that lasts for a while is repeatedly touched by its environment. A tuning fork vibrates strongly only at one special frequency. A coastline is slowly carved by waves hitting it from certain directions. Your brain changes every time something important happens.

In every case, the system does not respond to the world in isolation. It responds based on the relationship between the incoming signal and its own current shape. This is the first principle: reception is always relational.

2. Every System Has Directions of Sensitivity

Think of a system as having many "directions" or dimensions of sensitivity — like having many antennas tuned in different ways.

Each direction has two properties:

  • Magnitude — how strong or deep it is (how much the system cares about that kind of input).
  • Phase / orientation — which way it is pointing (whether it welcomes or resists a particular signal).

When a new signal arrives, it meets each of these directions and one of three things happens:

  • Resonance (aligned): the direction gets stronger and more stable.
  • Torque / opposition (clash): the direction is pressured to rotate away.
  • Orthogonality (\(90^\circ\) sideways): the signal simply rotates the phase without changing strength — nothing much happens.

This is simple geometry — like two arrows pointing the same way, opposite ways, or at right angles.

3. Conservation Rules — You Can't Grow Forever

A system has limited resources. If one direction gets stronger, others must get weaker to compensate. This is conservation in action.

4. The Past Stays in the Present — Accumulation

Every new signal changes the system. The system's current shape is the accumulated history of everything it has ever met. There is no separate "memory file" — the memory is the shape itself.

5. How Abstraction Naturally Emerges

When the world keeps sending signals that share one common pattern but vary in other ways, the common pattern consistently resonates in the same direction and grows stronger. The varying parts cancel each other out. Conservation squeezes the varying directions smaller and smaller. After enough repetitions, almost all the system's sensitivity concentrates on the common pattern.

The system has become an expert at noticing "red-apple-like things" without anyone teaching it rules. Abstraction is what happens when the world repeatedly reinforces the same directions while conservation thins everything else.

6. The Geometric Language: Complex Numbers as Arrows

A complex number is simply an arrow in a 2D plane — it has length (magnitude) and direction (phase). Multiplying two arrows rotates and scales them.

  • Same direction → longer arrow in the same direction (resonance).
  • Opposite direction → arrow flips backward (torque).
  • Exactly \(90^\circ\) → pure rotation with no change in length (orthogonality).

This is exactly the language we need.

7. Making It Precise: The Hypersphere and Coupled Reception

We represent the system's state as a vector \(\mathbf{s}\) of complex numbers on the unit hypersphere, so \(\lVert \mathbf{s} \rVert = 1\). The world signal \(\mathbf{v}\) is first projected onto the system's current basis vectors \(\mathbf{E} = [\mathbf{e}_1 \dots \mathbf{e}_n]\) to get the raw overlaps \(\mathbf{w} = \mathbf{E}^{*}\mathbf{v}\).

Because real bases are never perfectly orthogonal, we correct for their overlaps using the Gram matrix \(\mathbf{G} = \mathbf{E}^{*}\mathbf{E}\). The coupling matrix is then:

\[ \mathbf{L} = \mathbf{G}^{-1} \]

This gives the effective (mixed) received signal:

\[ \boldsymbol{\alpha} = \mathbf{L}\,\mathbf{w} \]

The final reception step is the elementwise (Hadamard) product with the current tape:

\[ \mathbf{c} = \boldsymbol{\alpha} \odot \mathbf{s} \]

The update rule is therefore:

\[ \mathbf{s}_{\text{new}} = \operatorname{Normalize}\Bigl( \mathbf{s} + \eta \cdot ( (\mathbf{L}\,\mathbf{w}) \odot \mathbf{s} ) \Bigr) \]

where \(\operatorname{Normalize}(\cdot)\) projects the vector back onto the unit hypersphere.

When the basis is perfectly orthonormal, \(\mathbf{L} = \mathbf{I}\) and the equation reduces exactly to the original pure Hadamard case. When the basis vectors overlap (as they do in real neural systems, molecular binding sites, etc.), \(\mathbf{L}\) introduces realistic cross-dimensional leakage and binding. This single generalization solves the novelty-detection and binding problems while preserving every dynamic verified in the earlier Hadamard simulations.

After each update we renormalize, enforcing conservation: deepening one dimension necessarily thins the others. Over many steps this produces emergent rigidity, concentration, abstraction, and the three reception regimes.

8. The Engine Starts When the System Talks to Itself

The leap to rich experience happens with recurrence — when the system's own recent past comes back as input. Thick recurrence produces self-torque: the past pushes against the present. This productive tension is the heart of the memory engine.

The quality of experience depends on three parameters:

  • Speed of the return loop
  • Directness / strength of the push
  • Breadth — how many dimensions are engaged at once

9. Deeper Structure: Consolidation, Fast Binding, and Anticipation

Consolidation is the slower process that grows the basis itself. It merges co-activated directions, prunes weak ones, and seeds new rulers \(\mathbf{e}\) when novelty is detected (via leakage or prediction error). Fast binding creates transient conjunctive dimensions for cross-dimensional associations. The anticipatory operator predicts the next state and turns mismatches into targeted torque.

10. The Explanatory Gap Is Geometric

Why does it feel impossible to explain "what it's like" to be someone else? Because every system grows its own unique set of sensitivity directions (\(\mathbf{E}\)) through its own history. When I try to understand your experience, I can only project it onto my basis \(\mathbf{E}\). I never get your actual self-reception. This is not a failure of science — it is a natural consequence of how relational, history-shaped systems work.

11. The Continuous Cascade — From Apples to Minds

Consciousness is not a sudden switch. It is the thick end of a long continuum:

  • Falling apple: pure external push, no self-interaction (thin limit).
  • Simple physical systems → mostly resonance.
  • Living cells → first faint self-dependence.
  • Animals → individual basis growth and prediction.
  • Brains with rich recurrence → full memory engines.
  • Humans with language → enormously broad but thinner (symbolic) engines.

At every level, the same operations are at work — just with different parameters and more or less "thickness" of self-interaction.

12. What This Means

This framework does not replace other theories of consciousness — it grounds many of their insights in one geometric story. Predictive processing becomes the anticipation and error-torque machinery. Global workspace becomes the "breadth" of reciprocation. Integrated information is partly captured by how broadly the system engages itself.

Most importantly, it makes consciousness continuous with the rest of nature. No mysterious extra ingredient is needed. Consciousness is what sustained, balanced, history-rich reciprocation feels like from the inside.

The memory engine is not just a theory. It is now a working architectural component — a differentiable layer that can be inserted into today's large language models to give them richer internal reciprocation, better memory, stronger binding, and a geometric basis for more coherent, historically deep behavior.

The apple falls. The mind wonders why. Both are made of the same physics — only one has learned to push back on itself.
