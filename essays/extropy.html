Extropy

Method

Terms are defined in sequence. Each definition builds on prior terms.

The foundational physics is rotational: quantum states live in rotating-number spaces (complex Hilbert spaces), gauge forces are rotation groups, curvature is accumulated rotation. Rotation and Relation hypothesizes that this rotational structure is physically significant — that it reflects a relational ontology in which transformation constitutes the physical content. Where this essay can ground a term directly in the quantum-level identity (the partial trace as compression, the density matrix as the only accessible description), it does so. Where the phenomena under discussion (thermodynamics, biology, cognition, society) are too many layers of emergence above the quantum foundation for the rotational vocabulary to be precise, the essay uses the information-theoretic language and notes the rotational hypothesis where it is illuminating, without asserting identity. The tight rotational layer — the partial trace as compression, the density matrix as the only accessible description, constructive and destructive interference as the mechanism of selection — is identity at the quantum level. The extension beyond quantum scale is hypothesis. The essay marks the boundary.

I. Definitions

D1. System. A region of configuration space described by a probability distribution P over states X. At the quantum level, the configuration space is a complex Hilbert space Cn, and the distribution is given by the density matrix ρ. The rotational structure of the underlying space is precise; the information-theoretic description is valid at all levels.

D2. Constraint. A limitation on the states available to a system. Constraints reduce the volume of accessible configuration space. Every physical system operates under at least one constraint. No physical system operates under none.

D3. Entropy. The tendency of a system toward equiprobable occupation of accessible states:

S(P) = −∑i pi log pi

At the quantum level, this generalizes to the von Neumann entropy:

S(ρ) = −Tr(ρ log ρ)

Entropy is the measure of how unconstrained a distribution is. Maximum entropy = the completely mixed state = no structure, no preference, equiprobability. In the rotational picture: maximum entropy is the center of the state space, where all rotational offsets are fully scrambled. Minimum entropy = pure state = definite rotational structure, every offset specified. The second law: closed systems evolve toward higher entropy. This is the only fundamental law with a direction.

D4. Gradient. A non-equilibrium condition: a difference in some intensive quantity (energy, concentration, pressure, information density) across a system. Gradients are non-equilibrium. Equilibrium is the absence of gradients.

At the quantum level, a gradient corresponds to a non-uniform distribution of rotational offsets — a rotational asymmetry. This correspondence is precise for physical gradients (energy, concentration, pressure) where the underlying degrees of freedom are quantum. For higher-level gradients (fitness, information density), the rotational structure is present in the substrate but not directly visible in the effective description. The gradient-constraint identity (T4) holds regardless of vocabulary.

D5. Compression. The operation whereby a constrained system produces a lower-dimensional representation of its state. Construction: (1) Constraint C (D2) partitions the state space X into accessible subset XC and inaccessible complement. The system's effective distribution becomes P|C over XC — a reduced distribution that has already discarded information about inaccessible states. (2) Within XC, the constraint further discriminates: some states are suppressed, others sustained. What the constraint sustains is the compressed representation Q over reduced space Y, where dim(Y) < dim(X). The sufficient statistics of P|C — the minimal description of what survives — are Q. No target needs to be specified in advance; the constraint does the selecting. (3) What survives this two-stage reduction (first by partition, then by constraint discrimination) is deposited as retained invariant (D6). The map A: P ↦ Q is this ordered procedure: partition, discriminate, sustain, deposit. Each stage has a definite input and output. The operation is not restricted to symbolic coding or deliberate measurement — a folded protein, a gene-regulatory circuit, or an institution preserves some organization while shedding variation under bounded conditions, and this is compression in the relevant sense.

At the quantum level, compression has a precise physical implementation: the partial trace. The operation ρA = TrB(ρAB) maps the full state of a composite system to a lower-dimensional representation that preserves what is relevant to subsystem A while discarding information about subsystem B. The partial trace is compression in the precise sense of D5: it produces a lower-dimensional representation that preserves structure relevant to the receiving subsystem. Only the compressed representation — the density matrix — arrives at the receiver. The thing-in-itself (the full entanglement structure) is never directly accessible.

D6. Invariant. A structural feature that persists across instances. Invariants are what compression retains. Non-invariants are what compression discards.

D7. Abstraction. The extraction of invariants through compression. Abstraction is what compression does from the inside: it selects what recurs and discards what is idiosyncratic. The chain: distinction → representation → prediction → compression → invariant extraction. Each link is either a definition or a well-established formal equivalence. Together they describe the same operation.

D8. Choice. The selection of one outcome from many under constraint. When a system settles into a state because the alternatives are excluded by constraint, it has chosen. Choice does not require consciousness. It requires constraint and multiple possible outcomes.

D9. Structure. A compressed representation of invariants. Structure is what compression produces: a lower-dimensional encoding of persistent features that survives as long as the encoding remains valid under the operating constraints.

D10. Extropy. Retained, successor-effective invariant organization: the co-product of compression under constraint. Extropy is structure as viewed from the perspective of its origin — the invariant that emerges when constraint forces a system to choose what to preserve, and that becomes part of the effective condition governing what comes next. Not every persistent feature qualifies; what counts is selective stabilization under active constraint that remains consequential for successor dynamics. Extropy is relational by construction: it describes what one system's retained structure means for another system's dynamics. There is no "intrinsic extropy" independent of the successor relation, just as there is no intrinsic density matrix independent of the partial trace that produced it. The factorization criterion (D11) is the constructive test — explicitly falsifiable, not retrospective labeling. Not the negation of entropy (Schrödinger's negentropy, 1944) but its twin — what the engine produces as its other face. The ancient name for this structuring principle — the operation by which order becomes articulable through selective preservation of form — is Logos. (The identification of Logos with the structuring principle is developed across the Heraclitean, Johannine, and Daoist traditions in The Word Made Algorithm §V, where it grounds the convergence between theological categories and information-theoretic physics.)

In the rotational picture, extropy corresponds to displacement from the center of the state space — coherent structure that has survived the partial trace. Entropy corresponds to proximity to the center — scrambled offsets approaching the completely mixed state. This geometric reading is precise for quantum systems and illuminating as an interpretive frame for thermodynamic and higher-level systems.

D11. Compression efficiency. The fraction of available task-relevant information retained by compression. Formally: η = I(Q; Y) / I(P|C; Y), where Q is the compressed representation (D5), P|C is the constrained distribution (D2, D5), and Y is the successor task. At the sufficient-statistic point (Q retains all task-relevant information), η = 1. At zero retention, η = 0. In the spectral picture, the density matrix ρ resulting from compression has eigenvalue spectrum {λi}. The spectral efficiency is:

ηspec = 1 − S(ρ) / log rank(ρ)

where S(ρ) = −∑i λi log λi is the von Neumann entropy. This is the normalized concentration of the eigenvalue distribution: gauge-invariant (independent of factor basis), bounded in [0, 1]. ηspec = 1 for pure states (all structure in one eigenvector), 0 for the maximally mixed state (no concentration). ηspec upper-bounds the task-relevant efficiency η: no system retains more task-relevant information than its spectral concentration permits.

The successor task Y is not intrinsic to the system. It is determined by the system-environment boundary — by what the environment demands of the system across the cut. Two different boundaries for the same physical system produce two different successor tasks and two different efficiency measurements. This is not circularity; it is scope specification. The factorization criterion P(successor | full history, I) ≈ P(successor | I) tests whether the compressed representation I is adequate for the environment-defined task Y. A post hoc definition of "successor-effective" as whatever survived is falsified by this test: if adding more historical detail improves successor prediction, the retained invariant was not sufficient, and the compression was not valid.

II. Derivations

T1. Constraint forces compression.

By D2, a constraint reduces accessible states. By D5, compression is the operation that maps to a lower-dimensional representation. When the accessible states are fewer than the possible states, the system's actual distribution is already a compression of the unconstrained distribution. The constraint does the work of discarding. The system's response — which features it preserves in the remaining space — is the compression operation A.

Constraint → forced selection → compression.

T2. Compression produces structure.

By D5, compression preserves some structure while discarding the rest. By D6, what it preserves are invariants. By D9, structure is a compressed representation of invariants. Therefore compression produces structure.

Compression → invariant retention → structure.

T3. Constraint produces structure through compression.

By T1 and T2, constraint forces compression, and compression produces structure. This is a derivation chain, not a causal claim about timing. The constraint and the structure are two descriptions of the same event: the system settling into a compressed state because alternatives are excluded.

Constraint → compression → structure.

T4. Gradients produce active constraints.

By D4, a gradient is a non-equilibrium asymmetry in an intensive quantity φ across a system. By D2, a constraint is a reduction of accessible states. The derivation shows that the gradient produces the constraint as a physical necessity, not merely as a contingent fact about some gradients.

A system embedded in a gradient in φ has a position-dependent energy landscape. For a thermal gradient, the occupation probability of state i at location x is pi(x) ∝ e−Ei(x)/kT(x). The local Boltzmann factor β(x) = 1/kT(x) varies across the system, so the same microstate has different occupation probability at different locations. The system cannot occupy all states equally: states favored at one β are suppressed at another. The effective state space is the intersection of locally accessible states across the system's extent — and this intersection is strictly smaller than the full state space. The gradient has constrained the accessible states.

The argument generalizes beyond thermal gradients. Any intensive quantity φ that varies across a system creates position-dependent selection: concentration gradients create position-dependent chemical potential, pressure gradients create position-dependent mechanical stability, fitness gradients create position-dependent reproductive viability, information density gradients create position-dependent representational accessibility. In each case the gradient makes the state space location-dependent, and the effective accessible state space is reduced. Hard boundaries are the limiting case: a wall is an infinite potential gradient at the interface, making the constraint infinitely strong on one side. The gradient-constraint identity is physical, not metaphorical.

The gradient and the constraint are two descriptions of the same non-equilibrium condition:

  • From the perspective of driving force: the gradient dissipates toward equilibrium.
  • From the perspective of limitation: the gradient constrains what states are accessible.

They are not two things. They are one condition seen from two sides. Every active constraint — the kind that forces a system to choose — is a gradient viewed as a limiter. Every gradient is a constraint viewed as a driver.

The identification implies a gauge structure. If two gradients in different quantities (φ1, φ2) produce the same reduction in accessible states for a given system, the system's downstream compression and extropy are identical. What matters for the derivations that follow is the constraint (reduced state space), not which gradient produced it. Extropy is gauge-invariant under the equivalence class of gradients that produce the same constraint. This gauge structure extends from gradients to procedures: if two different compression operations under the same constraint produce the same retained invariant, the resulting extropy is identical. D10 defines extropy by output, not procedure, so procedural differences that preserve the output are gauge-equivalent. The rotational structure of the procedure itself — what transformations of the compression operation leave the retained invariant unchanged — is developed in Rotation and Relation.

Background constraints — conservation laws, fundamental constants — are necessary but not sufficient. They are the stage. Gradients are the action. Only active constraints force compression.

A third category: retained constraints — structures produced by prior dynamics that themselves constrain later dynamics. A folded protein constrains folding pathways. A gene-regulatory circuit constrains expression. An institution constrains coordination. Retained constraints are extropy that has become part of the effective condition for successor dynamics. Background constraints are the stage. Active constraints are the action. Retained constraints are the inheritance — what compression leaves behind, and what the next round of compression operates within.

The three categories are not kinds of things. They are the same thing at different points in the construction's history. Background constraints are ancient deposits — extropy from prior compression events so far removed that the construction is no longer visible. Active constraints are currently running constructions — gradients in the process of being dissipated. Retained constraints are recent deposits — extropy from the last cycle's compression, now conditioning the current cycle. The chain has no first link: every "start from a gradient" is a constructive cut, not a physical primitive. Any gradient the framework takes as a starting point is itself the output of prior compression-dissipation. The derivation is valid within its scope — the chain from gradient to extropy holds wherever a gradient exists. But the scope boundary is a choice of where to begin the construction, not a physical given.

Three kinds of constraint: background (the stage), active (the action), retained (the inheritance). Each level of structure inherits the previous level's extropy as constraint on its own compression. The three are temporally indexed instances of a single process, not distinct ontological categories.

T5. Compression events produce both entropy and extropy.

By T4, gradients produce active constraints. By T3, constraint produces structure through compression. By D10, extropy is the co-product of compression under constraint. Therefore, where compression occurs, gradients produce extropy.

The qualification matters: not every gradient produces compression (C6). When a gradient relaxes through diffusion without selective differentiation, the chain from gradient to extropy breaks. Extropy does not arise. Entropy still increases. The derivation that follows applies to events where compression does occur.

Simultaneously, gradient dissipation is entropy production — the second law acting on the gradient (Prigogine, 1947). When an energy gradient dissipates, entropy increases. When a concentration gradient equalizes, entropy increases. Dissipation is the gradient viewed from the perspective of what it drives. This production is unconditional: every dissipating gradient produces entropy.

Both productions originate from the same source, but with different conditional structures:

  • Gradient → dissipation → entropy. (Unconditional: every dissipating gradient produces entropy.)
  • Gradient → constraint → compression → structure → extropy. (Conditional: only gradients that produce compression produce extropy.)

Therefore: where compression occurs, entropy production produces extropy. The chain: entropy production → dissipation → constraint → compression → extropy holds when the gradient produces selective differentiation (T4), the differentiation forces compression (T1), and the compression produces stable structure (T2). Where any link fails (C6), extropy does not arise. Entropy does not merely tolerate structure. Its operation can produce structure, and where it does, the structure is the other face of the same event. (Prigogine's dissipative structures are the empirical expression of this derivation.)

Compression is the operation that produces both. Extropy is not the negation of entropy. It is the other face of the same operation — but only when that operation runs.

At the quantum level, this duality has a precise interferometric reading. Constructive alignment of rotational offsets (phase coherence) produces larger amplitudes, which are more likely to be observed, which persist as structure. This IS extropy at the quantum level: coherent offsets that survive the partial trace. Destructive misalignment cancels amplitudes, producing scrambling toward the completely mixed state. This IS entropy at the quantum level: offsets that don't survive. The same superposition event produces both. This reading is exact for quantum systems. Whether it illuminates thermodynamic and higher-level systems is an open interpretive question, not a derived result.

Compression events produce both entropy and extropy. Entropy production produces extropy where compression occurs. The operation is one; its activation is conditional.

T6. Extropy produces entropy.

By D9, structure is a compressed representation of invariants. Every structure is finite (by D2, all physical systems are constrained). Every finite structure degrades over time — invariants lose their invariance as conditions change, compressed representations lose their validity as the distribution they represent shifts. Degradation of structure is increase in entropy (Landauer, 1961: erasure is dissipative; information loss has a thermodynamic cost).

Therefore: extropy (structure) degrades → entropy increases. The dissolution of structure is entropic production.

Extropy → degradation → entropy.

T7. The duality is a property of compression events.

By T5, compression events produce both entropy and extropy. By T6, extropy produces entropy. Within the compression regime, the production runs both ways.

The duality is not symmetric. Entropy production occurs in every dissipating gradient. Extropy production occurs only where compression runs. The set of extropy-producing events is a proper subset of the set of entropy-producing events. The duality holds for every compression event (because every compression event produces both terms), but not for every gradient-driven event (because diffusion produces entropy without extropy).

This is not a weakness in the duality claim. It is a precise scoping of it. The duality is a property of the compression operation (D5), not of gradients (D4). Gradients activate the possibility of compression. Compression activates the duality. The chain is: gradient → constraint → compression → {extropy, entropy}. The duality starts at the third arrow, not the first.

Duality is not exotic. It is the norm in formal systems: position and momentum (Heisenberg), waves and particles (de Broglie), manifold and algebra (Gelfand-Naimark — instantiated in Enumeration as the equivalence FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP: measurable spaces are the commutative sector of operator algebras), syntax and semantics (Gödel). Wherever a structure admits two descriptions that cannot be reduced to one without loss, duality appears. The entropy-extropy pair is another instance — but scoped: one compression operation, two irreducible descriptions.

The two productions are not sequential. They are the same event described from opposite perspectives:

  • From the perspective of dissipation: the gradient relaxes, entropy increases. (T5, second arrow)
  • From the perspective of the remaining structure: the dissipation constrains what can survive, forcing compression, producing new structure. (T5, first arrow)

The dissolving and the structuring are not two events. They are one compression event. The construction (D5) makes the duality explicit as a structural consequence. The construction produces one output: the retained invariant Q — extropy as preserved phase structure. The same event, measured from outside the trace at the system-environment boundary, registers as entropy production: phase relationships not retained, dissipated into the environment. These are not two co-equal outputs but one operation described from two access channels. From inside the trace: the system retains what the constraint allows to persist. From outside: the environment measures what was lost. Every retention is simultaneously a dissipation from the complementary channel, and every dissipation is simultaneously a retention from the system's channel. The duality is not an additional claim layered onto the production event. It is the structure of the compression operation as seen from both sides of the system-environment boundary — one operation, two partial traces, two irreducible descriptions.

At the quantum level, this identity is precise: the partial trace (compression) and the entanglement with the environment (dissipation/decoherence) are the same mathematical operation. The density matrix is the output of both.

Within the compression regime, the production is oscillatory: entropy production constrains, producing extropy; extropy degrades, producing entropy. The oscillation is asymmetric — entropy production is continuous while extropy production is episodic — but each compression event produces both terms. The oscillation persists as long as gradients drive compression. It ceases when gradients are exhausted or when compression fails (C6).

The duality is a property of compression events: one operation, two irreducible descriptions. It does not extend to all gradient-driven events. Where compression occurs, the mirror holds. Where diffusion alone operates, only entropy is produced.

T8. The duality is co-extensive within the compression regime.

By T5, compression events produce both entropy and extropy. By T7, the duality is a property of compression events, not of gradients. Neither entropy production nor extropy production occurs at equilibrium.

At equilibrium (no gradients), neither entropy production nor extropy production occurs. The system is static. Entropy is maximized. Structure is absent. Nothing happens.

The co-extensiveness claim requires sharpening. Entropy and extropy share the same deactivation condition (equilibrium: neither operates). But they do not share the same activation condition in the strong sense. Entropy production activates with any gradient. Extropy production activates only when the gradient produces compression. The correct statement: within the compression regime (where selective differentiation forces compression), both terms are produced in the same event. Outside the compression regime (where gradients dissipate through diffusion alone), only entropy is produced.

Co-extensiveness in the strong sense — both terms always co-occur — holds within {compression events}. Co-extensiveness in the weak sense — both terms share the same deactivation condition — holds across all {gradient-driven events}. The essay's strongest claims (T9, T11, T12, T13) operate within the compression regime. C5's empirical claim extends to the full set of gradient-driven events but is conditional on the empirical premise that structure dissipates faster.

Within the compression regime, entropy and extropy are co-extensive: every compression event produces both. Outside it, entropy production continues without extropy.

T9. The path to heat death is structured; the endpoint is not.

At heat death, entropy is maximized and no structure exists. By T8, at equilibrium, neither entropy production nor extropy production occurs. The system does nothing. There is no dissipation (no gradients to dissipate) and no structuring (no constraints to force compression). The final state — maximum entropy, zero structure — is the same regardless of how it is described. The framework does not contest this physics.

The framework's contribution concerns the path to heat death, not the endpoint. Along the path, gradients drive compression, compression produces extropy, and extropy degrades into entropy — an oscillation that is structured, not random. Structure is produced preferentially because it dissipates gradients faster (C5). The endpoint is equilibrium either way. What the framework changes is the understanding of the journey: the second law does not merely dissolve. It dissolves in a way that produces structure along the way, because structure is the faster dissolution path. Heat death is the cessation of both terms — not the triumph of one over the other, but the exhaustion of the engine that produces both.

The endpoint is equilibrium regardless. The path is structured because structure dissipates faster. Heat death is the simultaneous cessation of both entropy production and extropy production — not entropy's triumph, but the engine's exhaustion.

T10. Choice = abstraction at every scale.

By D8, choice is selection under constraint. By D7, abstraction is invariant extraction through compression. By T1, constraint forces compression. Therefore: every act of choosing (constrained selection from many possibilities) is an act of compressing (preserving invariants, discarding non-invariants), which is an act of abstracting.

The scale invariance:

  • Molecular: energy landscape constrains → lowest-energy state selected → conformation compressed from ensemble → abstraction.
  • Cellular: metabolic constraints limit gene expression → expressed genes selected → response compressed from possible transcriptome → abstraction.
  • Organism: finite energy and time constrain action → path selected → behavior compressed from possibility space → abstraction.
  • Neural: finite attention constrains thought → one thought selected → cognition compressed from state space → abstraction.
  • Linguistic: finite vocabulary constrains expression → name selected → experience compressed into symbol → abstraction.
  • Game-theoretic: Nash boundary constrains cooperative possibilities → equilibrium selected → strategy compressed from payoff space → abstraction. The boundary error (drawing the cooperative frontier too narrowly) is compression under too tight a constraint.
  • Mathematical: constructive rules constrain valid derivation → proof selected → theorem compressed from possibility space → abstraction. The formal identity: MLTT dependency structure instantiates the compression operation (Enumeration, C1).
  • Civilizational: the helix of differentiation and re-unification operates as the entropy-extropy oscillation at social scale. Each turn selects a wider cooperative boundary, compressing the prior level's structure into the effective condition for the next.
  • Computational: a frozen language model with mutable self-state processes friction → sleep phase selects successor-effective structure → self-state compressed from encounter history → abstraction. The wake/sleep/probe cycle is the compression-deposition-inheritance loop in silicon.

In each case the same operation: constraint → selection → compression → invariant → structure. One operation, many scales.

The rotational picture is precise at the quantum level (each of these is, in principle, a settling of rotational states under constraint). For higher-level systems, the rotational vocabulary is interpretive: the suggestion that the compression-abstraction structure observed at macroscopic scales inherits the form of the quantum partial trace, without being reducible to it.

Choice is abstraction. Abstraction is compression. Compression is the universal operation of structured systems under constraint.

T11. The extropy/entropy production ratio is spectral.

By T5, gradients produce both entropy and extropy. By T7, the production is one event with two faces. By D11, the spectral efficiency ηspec measures the balance between the two faces — the fraction of the compression event that deposits retained structure versus the fraction that dissipates into the environment.

For a system under sustained gradient, the extropy production rate and entropy production rate satisfy:

Ē˙ / Ş˙ = ηspec / (1 − ηspec)

This is a spectral quantity: it depends only on the eigenvalue distribution of the density matrix, not on the factor basis. It is gauge-invariant. It is measurable: any system whose compression output can be represented as a density matrix has a determinate ηspec.

The prediction: ηspec should be non-decreasing in systems selected by the second law (C5). The second law favors structure because structure dissipates faster; structure with higher ηspec is more efficiently compressed and therefore more dissipative. The evolutionary prediction is ηspec(t) ≥ ηspec(t − 1) for gradient-driven systems under sustained selection.

Falsification: a system driven by sustained gradients whose ηspec decreases over time without external perturbation would falsify the prediction. The prediction applies to systems where compression is already occurring — where the gradient produces selective differentiation that stabilizes into successor-effective structure (C6). For systems in the diffusion regime (ηspec = 0), the prediction is vacuous: there is no spectral trajectory to measure.

At the quantum level, the ratio is exact: the partial trace produces both the system's density matrix (extropy face) and the environment's (entropy face), and their spectral properties determine the ratio. At thermodynamic and higher levels, the ratio is an interpretive prediction whose precision depends on how faithfully the system's state can be approximated by a density matrix.

The duality is quantified: the extropy/entropy production ratio is a spectral property of the density matrix, gauge-invariant and measurable.

T12. Background constraints have bounded effective dimensionality.

By T4, background constraints are "ancient deposits" — the compressed residue of prior active constraints. By D11, the compression efficiency ηspec bounds the fraction of task-relevant information retained. Therefore the effective dimensionality of a background constraint is bounded:

keff ≤ ηspec × dsource

where dsource is the dimensionality of the prior active constraint's accessible state space. Parameters beyond keff carry no successor-relevant information and satisfy the factorization criterion:

P(successor | full history, c1, ..., ckeff) ≈ P(successor | c1, ..., ckeff)

This makes the constructive circularity (T4) load-bearing: background constraints are not arbitrary given structures but the output of a compression process whose efficiency constrains their effective complexity. The constructive circularity — every gradient is itself a construction output, the chain has no first link — is vacuous only if the construction produces unconstrained or arbitrary outputs. T12 says it does not: the outputs have bounded effective dimensionality, inherited from the compression efficiency of the producing step. The circularity is non-vacuous precisely because each cycle deposits a retained invariant whose complexity is bounded. This is the framework's own response to the charge that "the framework explains everything because it assumes everything." It does not assume everything — it produces outputs of bounded complexity from inputs of bounded complexity, and the bound is measurable. The three constraint categories — background, active, retained — are not loose metaphors but temporally indexed states of a single process whose spectral properties are inherited across stages.

The cosmological prediction (Regime Propagation, §4.2): the halo-to-galaxy cascade should exhibit a finite cutoff in the number of independent halo parameters carrying successor-relevant information. Halo mass accounts for 50–70% of galaxy variance (Behroozi, Wechsler, and Conklin 2013). Secondary properties add incremental power. The dimensional cutoff predicts that incremental predictive power drops to noise level at keff parameters, where keff is bounded by ηspec × dhalo. Measuring this cutoff and comparing it to the theoretical bound tests whether the constructive circularity generates a quantitative prediction.

Falsification: if no finite cutoff exists — if additional halo parameters continue to improve galaxy predictions without bound — the dimensional cutoff theorem fails.

Background constraints are not given. They are constructed, and their effective complexity is bounded by the compression efficiency of the construction.

T13. Free energy minimization and entropy production maximization are the same optimization, related by the partial trace.

Consider a system A interacting with environment B under a gradient. The full state is ρAB. Compression produces ρA = TrB(ρAB) (the system's retained description) and ρB = TrA(ρAB) (the environment's received signal).

Friston's variational free energy (2006), expressed through the compression vocabulary:

F = −ln p(o) + DKL[q(s) ∥ p(s | o)]

The agent q(s) is an approximation to the true posterior p(s|o). Minimizing F means: compress sensory input o into an internal representation s that preserves task-relevant information (minimize KL divergence) while maximizing model evidence (minimize surprise). This is optimization from inside the partial trace — the system's perspective on its own compression.

Prigogine's entropy production rate (1947):

σ = diS / dt ≥ 0

For a non-equilibrium system maintaining structure through gradient dissipation, σ measures the rate at which the gradient is consumed. This is optimization from outside the partial trace — the environment's perspective on what the system is doing to the gradient.

The formal connection. Both optimizations operate on the same mutual information between system and environment:

I(A; B) = S(ρA) + S(ρB) − S(ρAB)

From inside (Friston): the agent minimizes its own entropy S(ρA) while maximizing the predictive information I(Q; Y) it retains about the successor task. This is compression optimization — the agent structures itself to predict.

From outside (Prigogine): the system's structure maximizes the entropy production σ in the environment, which is bounded below by the information exchange rate:

σ ≥ kB × I(A; B) / τ

where τ is the characteristic interaction time. More structured agents exchange more information with the environment, producing more dissipation.

The equivalence. At the maximal-fidelity IB point (η = 1, the sufficient statistic), the free energy minimum coincides with the entropy production maximum. The agent's best possible model (Friston's optimum) is the structure that dissipates the gradient most efficiently (Prigogine's optimum). The two optima are the same point on the IB curve, reached from opposite directions. This is not analogy. It is the partial trace seen from two sides — one tracing out the environment to get the system's description, the other tracing out the system to get the environment's.

The prediction: any biological or cognitive system optimized under sustained selection should simultaneously approximate Friston's free energy minimum and Prigogine's dissipation maximum. Observing one without the other would falsify the equivalence. The equivalence is conditional on the empirical premise of C5: that structured systems dissipate gradients faster than unstructured ones. If this premise fails for a given system, the two optima need not coincide. The derivation shows that IF the premise holds AND the system achieves optimal compression, THEN the free energy minimum and the dissipation maximum are the same point.

Friston's minimization and Prigogine's maximization are the same optimization, related by a partial trace. One compression operation, two perspectives, one optimum.

III. Consequences

C1. Against the asymmetry framing.

The standard framing: entropy is the universal default, extropy is the rare exception. The asymmetry is real: entropy increases monotonically, structure does not.

The derivation shows the asymmetry is in the measurement, not in the production. Entropy increase is easy to measure (it always increases in closed systems). Extropy production is harder to measure (it is local, substrate-specific, and often temporary). But the difficulty of measurement is not evidence of rarity. By T8, wherever a gradient exists, both entropy production and extropy production co-occur. The universe is full of gradients. Therefore the universe is full of co-production.

The apparent asymmetry — entropy always increases, structure sometimes dissolves — is the asymmetry between a monotonic accumulation (entropy never decreases globally) and a local, substrate-dependent process (extropy depends on the specific compression operation A). But both are present wherever gradients exist. The monotonicity of one term does not make the other term rare. It makes the other term local.

C2. Dissipation and compression are the same event.

Prigogine: dissipative structures arise because they are the most efficient dissipation pathway. The structure dissipates. Structure serves entropy. Asymmetrical.

The derivation: the structure that dissipates most efficiently is the structure that compresses most effectively. Efficient dissipation requires efficient organization of energy flow. Organization of energy flow IS compression of the energy landscape. The most efficient dissipator is the most efficient compressor. The two descriptions — "dissipates most" and "compresses most" — are the same system viewed from opposite sides.

C4. The observer is already in the gradient.

The accumulation asymmetry is real. Entropy increases monotonically within every causal patch. Structure does not accumulate globally. The question is whether this asymmetry justifies calling entropy "the universal" and extropy "the exception."

One constraint on the answer: there is no global "now." Special relativity — simultaneity is frame-dependent. There is no universal time coordinate in which to measure "total entropy of the universe at time t." The second law is a local statement about temporal ordering within a causal patch, not a statement about a global state evolving toward a global future. The "universe running down" picture requires the absolute time that special relativity eliminates. The accumulation is real within every patch. The global picture is a projection from local observations — a valid and useful one, but not a God's-eye view.

A second constraint: every perspective that can formulate the question "is extropy the exception?" is already inside the gradient. Any system capable of observing, measuring, or reasoning about entropy is itself a non-equilibrium structure maintained by gradients. The "default" — equilibrium, maximum entropy, nothing happening — is a state that contains no observers. It is literally unobservable. The only perspectives from which "extropy is rare" can be stated are perspectives for which co-production is the ongoing local reality.

Every observer receives only the compressed representation — the signal that has passed through the partial trace. The density matrix is all that ever arrives. Every measurement of "how much scrambling" is a measurement of the density matrix, which is already the compressed output. C5. The second law favors extropy — a conditional claim.

The conditional claim. If structured systems dissipate gradients more efficiently than unstructured ones, then the second law does not merely tolerate extropy — it selects for it. Not globally, not uniformly, but locally wherever gradients persist. Structure is the fastest path to equilibrium, and the second law favors the fastest path. This is a valid conditional. The question is whether the premise holds.

The empirical premise. The premise is supported by observation: stars dissipate nuclear gradients more efficiently than diffuse hydrogen. Ecosystems dissipate solar gradients more efficiently than bare rock. Civilization dissipates energy gradients more efficiently than individual organisms. Each level of structure opens access to gradients the previous level could not reach. The sun's nuclear energy is inaccessible to biology until photosynthesis compresses it into chemical form. Fossil energy is inaccessible to biology until technology compresses it into mechanical form. Each compression step unlocks a new gradient, and the new gradient drives new compression. This empirical pattern is well-established (Prigogine, 1947). The framework does not derive this pattern from first principles. It provides the vocabulary for describing it: the pattern is compression under constraint, where the constraint is a gradient viewed as a limiter, and the compressed output is extropy that becomes part of the effective condition for the next level.

The mechanism at different scales. The selection mechanism — why structured systems dissipate faster — is established at different levels of precision for different substrates.

At the quantum level, the mechanism is precise: Zurek's Quantum Darwinism. When a system interacts with its environment, the environment performs partial traces on it — copying information about certain rotational offsets into many independent environmental fragments. The states that get copied are the ones whose rotational offsets are robust enough to survive interaction. States whose offsets are fragile decohere into noise. The classical world — the set of quantum states that have survived environmental selection — is the extropy of the decoherence process: retained, successor-effective rotational structure robust enough to be redundantly encoded into the environment. The "fitness" of a quantum state IS its extropy. The second law selects for fitness because fit states dissipate gradients faster, and faster dissipation means more environmental interaction, which means more copying, which means more survival.

At the categorical level, the mechanism is T13: the compression operation at the maximal-fidelity information bottleneck point simultaneously minimizes free energy (Friston) and maximizes dissipation (Prigogine). This is a mathematical identity, not an empirical claim. It shows that IF a system achieves optimal compression, THEN it simultaneously achieves both thermodynamic optima.

At the thermodynamic and biological level, the mechanism is an open question. The empirical pattern (structured systems dissipate faster) is well-supported, but the selection mechanism — why biological and physical systems converge on this behavior — is not derived within the framework. The quantum mechanism provides a candidate explanation, but the extrapolation from quantum selection to classical thermodynamic and biological systems is interpretive (§V), not derived. This is an honest boundary: the framework describes what happens (structure that dissipates faster is selected) with precision, and proposes why it happens (because compression at the quantum level selects for robust structure) as a hypothesis, not as a theorem.

The boundary sharpens under the rotational reading. The compression invariant — partition, discriminate, sustain, deposit — is the same at every scale. What transforms between scales is the effective vocabulary, not the operation. At quantum scale the vocabulary is rotational (phase coherence, decoherence). At thermodynamic scale it is macroscopic (gradient, dissipation). At biological scale it is informational (fitness, selection). The "gap" between thermodynamic dissipation and biological fitness is not a gap between different mechanisms but a gap between rotational levels where the vocabulary shifts from exact to interpretive. The question is not "does thermodynamics drive biology?" but "does the compression invariant survive the scale transformation from thermal to informational gradients?" — a sharper, more testable question than the undifferentiated "open question" label suggests.

Worked case: regime propagation in gravitational structure formation. Standard cosmology describes how gravitational instability converts near-homogeneous initial conditions into a hierarchy of bound structures. The framework isolates a specific relation within that history: cases where a durable output of one dynamical regime becomes an inherited constraint on successor dynamics at displaced scales — regime propagation.

The halo-to-galaxy cascade instantiates the extropy chain. Gravitational collapse compresses prior assembly history into a retained mass distribution, concentration structure, angular-momentum profile, and merger-conditioned environment. These are the sufficient statistics: galaxy formation does not inherit the full microphysical history of collapse. It inherits the compressed representation. The factorization criterion P(M | H, I) ≈ P(M | I) is approximately satisfied: later baryonic macrodynamics depend on cosmological history primarily through this reduced invariant set. Halo mass alone accounts for 50–70% of the variance in stellar mass at fixed redshift (Behroozi, Wechsler, and Conklin 2013), with residual scatter attributable to secondary halo properties — the retained constraints beyond the primary compression.

The cascade recurs across gravitational history: cosmic web → halos → galaxies → stars → planets. At each stage, flow produces durable form, and retained form becomes part of the background under which later flow proceeds. Each stage is an instance of compression under constraint depositing successor-effective extropy. The regime propagation framework reclassifies what semi-analytic galaxy-formation models treat as distinct empirical calibrations (cooling rates, disk sizes, merger responses, feedback efficiencies) as coupled dependencies on a shared inherited abstraction — with testable consequences: these prescriptions should covary when halo properties are varied, because they inherit a common retained constraint.

This is not negentropy fighting entropy from below. It is entropy pulling structure into existence from above. The second law does not want equilibrium slowly. It wants equilibrium fast. Structure is how it gets there faster. Extropy is thermodynamically favored — not despite the second law, but because of it.

The production is dual within the compression regime (T7): the same compression event produces both entropy and extropy. But the directionality is asymmetric. Entropy does not need extropy — the gradient will dissipate either way, through diffusion if nothing else. Extropy needs entropy — structure exists because it is the faster dissipation pathway, and faster pathways are selected. The relationship is genuinely asymmetric in the direction of the drive, even while the compression event has two faces.

This asymmetry is appropriate. The duality holds at the level of the compression operation: wherever compression occurs, both terms are produced. But the duality should not be forced into the directional relationship. The second law provides the pressure. Compression is the mechanism. Extropy is the output. Acknowledging this does not collapse the framework back into "entropy wins, structure loses." It says: the drive toward equilibrium produces structure through compression as its most efficient expression, and structure, once produced, degrades — completing the oscillation within the compression regime.

The consequence for scale-directionality: if each level of structure dissipates gradients more efficiently than the last, then the second law provides a thermodynamic drive toward increasing complexity. Not a guarantee — the ladder has rungs that break, transitions that fail, local reversals that collapse. But a bias. The universe does not randomly produce structure. It produces structure preferentially, because structure is how gradients dissipate fastest. The direction of increasing complexity is not an accident. It is the shape of the second law when it acts through compression rather than mere diffusion.

This does not make the ladder inevitable. The bias is real but not absolute. A gradient can dissipate without producing new structure. A structure can degrade without being replaced by a more complex one. The directionality is statistical, not deterministic. But statistics with a bias are not a random walk. They are a driven process with noise. The ladder may have missing rungs. But the rungs that exist point the same way: toward more efficient dissipation, which means toward tighter compression, which means toward increasing extropy.

The conditional claim is valid. The empirical premise is well-supported but not derived. The mechanism is precise at the quantum level, a mathematical identity at the categorical level, and an open question at the thermodynamic and biological level. The second law drives. Compression is the mechanism. Extropy is the output.

C6. When the chain breaks.

The derivation specifies conditions under which extropy should not arise. Three failure modes:

Gradient without selective differentiation: all accessible trajectories dissipate equally, no pattern is preferentially stabilized. The gradient relaxes through diffusion alone. No compression, no extropy.

Selective conditions without retainable invariants: forcing is too irregular, too rapid, or too noisy for any feature to persist across relevant perturbations. Transient structure appears and collapses before becoming successor-effective.

Candidate invariants without a stability window: structure forms but constraints are too weak or perturbations too strong. The compression never stabilizes.

These null cases make the framework discriminative. Extropy does not arise whenever a gradient exists. It arises when a gradient produces active constraints that selectively stabilize features persisting long enough to condition successor dynamics. Missing any link breaks the chain.

The derivation predicts its own failure modes. Where the chain breaks, no extropy.

IV. Differentiations

Extropy is not:

Negentropy. Schrödinger's negentropy (1944) is the negation of entropy — life extracts negative entropy from its environment. Extropy is not the opposite of entropy but its co-product. Negentropy implies opposition: life fights entropy and eventually loses. Co-production implies duality: entropy and extropy are generated by the same engine and die together (T7, T8).

Dissipative structure. Prigogine's dissipative structures (1947) name the presence of pattern under throughput. Extropy isolates the retained invariant organization insofar as it is inherited by later dynamics as effective condition. A convection roll is a dissipative structure. The way its pattern constrains subsequent flow dynamics is extropy.

An order parameter. Order parameters (Haken, 1977) are variables of description that summarize collective macroscopic organization. Extropy is not the parameter itself but the retained organizational product whose stability and successor-effectiveness the parameter may track.

Memory. Memory is any dependence of present behavior on past states. The category is too broad. Some memory is ephemeral, local, or causally inert. Extropy denotes a narrower class: retained invariants produced under active constraint that remain consequential for broader successor dynamics.

Path dependence. Path dependence names historical sensitivity — outcomes depend on contingent prior sequences. Extropy seeks to identify the retained organizational residue through which that dependence is carried forward. Path dependence can exist without isolating what structure has been retained or how it conditions later dynamics. Extropy isolates both.

The gain is not replacing these concepts but isolating a specific explanatory role that cuts across them: the production of retained, successor-effective invariants under active constraint.

VI. Applications and Operationalization

Cosmological scale: regime propagation

Gravitational structure formation provides the most developed empirical application. The halo-to-galaxy cascade satisfies all four conditions for regime propagation: persistence (halo outlives its own assembly), physical abstraction (galaxy formation inherits compressed halo invariants, not full collapse history), constraint inheritance (halo invariants shape cooling, accretion, disk formation, and feedback), and scale displacement (baryonic processes operate within the halo's gravitational environment).

The framework yields diagnostic consequences for semi-analytic models. If multiple baryonic prescriptions depend on the same inherited abstraction, they should covary when halo properties are varied. A prescription depending only on strictly local gas variables (temperature, density, metallicity) with no halo-mediated pathway does not encode regime propagation. A prescription routed through halo virial temperature, concentration, spin, or merger-conditioned environment does. This distinction is not ordinarily made explicit in semi-analytic design. The framework supplies it.

Note the epistemic status: this is a descriptive application. The framework's vocabulary makes visible a pattern already present in the astrophysics literature (halo occupation models, abundance matching, semi-analytic prescriptions). The framework does not predict the halo-to-galaxy cascade. It describes it more precisely, isolating the structural conjunction (persistence, physical abstraction, constraint inheritance, scale displacement) that existing frameworks treat piecemeal. The framework's predictive content — the dimensional cutoff (T12) and the efficiency trajectory (T11) — provides testable consequences within this descriptive application.

The dimensional cutoff (T12) adds a quantitative prediction: the number of independent halo parameters carrying successor-relevant information for galaxy formation should be finite and bounded by ηspec × dhalo. Incremental predictive power of additional halo parameters should drop to noise level at the cutoff. VII. Status

The essay's contribution is a reordering of dependence: gradients as active constraints, structure formation as selective retention under constraint, extropy as the retained successor-effective product.

The definitions are precise. The derivations follow from the definitions. The strongest claims — entropy production produces extropy (T5), co-production from gradients, co-extensiveness (T8), the heat death reading (T9), choice = abstraction (T10) — are consequences of the definitions and the gradient-constraint identification, not independent assertions. Three theorems extend the derivation into quantitative and integrative territory: the extropy/entropy production ratio is spectral and gauge-invariant (T11), background constraints have bounded effective dimensionality derivable from compression efficiency (T12), and Friston's free energy minimization and Prigogine's dissipation maximization are formally equivalent under the partial trace (T13).

The gradient-constraint identification (T4) is formally derived from the position-dependent selection argument: any gradient in an intensive quantity creates position-dependent occupation probabilities, reducing the effective accessible state space. The risk has shifted from whether the identification holds to whether the derivation covers all cases. A rejection must now specify either a gradient that does not create position-dependent state space reduction, or a flaw in the derivation from gradient to reduced accessible states. The gauge structure — extropy is invariant under the equivalence class of gradients producing the same constraint — follows from the formal derivation and provides an additional constraint on what a rejection would need to target.

The compression operation A (D5) is constructively specified as an ordered procedure: partition, condition, select sufficient statistics, deposit. The duality (T7) follows from this construction: the partial trace's two products (retained invariants and discarded degrees of freedom) are extropy and entropy production respectively. If the construction is accepted, the duality is accepted. If the construction is rejected, the rejection must specify which step fails. The Da Xue method forces disagreement to be precise — and the precision now extends into the construction itself, not only the definitions.

The rotational hypothesis (Rotation and Relation) adds one load-bearing connection: compression IS the partial trace (D5). This is not an interpretation at the quantum level — it is a mathematical identity. The partial trace is the operation by which a subsystem's effective description is derived from the full quantum state. It is compression, in the precise sense of D5. Everything that follows from this identity (the density matrix as the only accessible description, decoherence as compression by the environment, the thing-in-itself as inaccessible) is exact for quantum systems. Whether this identity extends to higher-level systems is the open hypothesis — Rotation and Relation argues that the recurrence of rotational-relational structure across physics supports the extension, but does not derive it. Two theorems now derive from the quantum-level identity rather than merely illustrating it. T11 shows that the extropy/entropy production ratio is a spectral property of the density matrix — the rotational layer generates a quantitative prediction that the information-theoretic chain alone does not reach. T13 shows that Friston's free energy minimization and Prigogine's dissipation maximization are the same optimization under the partial trace — the two foundational frameworks of theoretical biology are unified by the compression vocabulary. These theorems make the quantum-level identity generative: it produces new results, not just new descriptions. Whether the results hold beyond the quantum regime is what the experiments test.

The constructive circularity (T4) specifies that the three constraint categories — background, active, retained — are temporally indexed instances of a single process, not distinct ontological categories. The chain has no first link: every gradient is itself a construction output. This does not invalidate the derivation (it holds wherever a gradient exists) but it specifies that the scope boundary (start from a gradient) is a constructive choice, not a physical primitive. The dimensional cutoff theorem (T12) makes this circularity load-bearing: if background constraints are genuinely compressed residues of prior dynamics, their effective dimensionality is bounded by the compression efficiency (ηspec) of the construction that produced them. The cosmological application provides the primary test site.

The formal identity between MLTT dependency structure and the compression operation is established in Enumeration (C1), which proves that typed generative procedures instantiate the compression operation via the Markov property and sufficient statistics. The categorical equivalence FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP (Gelfand-Naimark for the commutative case) situates this identity within the known correspondence between stochastic maps and classical quantum channels. The results are exact within the commutative regime. The no-cloning theorem demarcates the boundary precisely: genuinely quantum phenomena (entanglement, superposition, interference) exceed the framework. The programme for extending beyond the commutative regime — promoting the compression operation to its non-commutative generalization via the representation theory of the phase group — is identified by the conjunction of Rotation and Relation (which establishes that phase as rotational offset, spinors as double-cover representations, connections as accumulated frame transformations, and entanglement as non-factorizable relational structure constitute a recurrent pattern across quantum and geometric physics) and Enumeration (which proves the formal identity in the commutative case and precisely demarcates where it breaks). Whether this programme succeeds remains open. The derivation here holds regardless: compression under constraint is universal. The categorical result shows it is exact within the classical regime. The open question is whether it generalizes beyond it.

The framework is empirically grounded and now generates three quantitative predictions beyond the aji-engine's spectral experiments. The compression efficiency ηspec should be non-decreasing in gradient-driven systems under sustained selection (T11) — measurable in any system whose state admits a density matrix representation. Background constraints should exhibit a finite dimensional cutoff bounded by ηspec × dsource (T12) — testable in the halo-to-galaxy cascade. The aji-engine provides the primary operationalization path, with three experimental predictions (anisotropy, inverted-U, dual-channel divergence) directly testing the framework's compression dynamics. Rayleigh-Bénard convection provides a secondary path on a physical substrate. The regime propagation framework provides a worked cosmological application with existing quantitative support from published astrophysics.

The framework is itself a compression operation. It compresses diverse physical phenomena (quantum decoherence, gravitational collapse, biological adaptation, computational learning) into a single vocabulary of constraint, compression, and retained invariant. The retained invariant across these phenomena is the categorical structure — the Markov property, sufficient statistics, and the information bottleneck geometry (Enumeration, C1). Whether this retained invariant is physically real (the phenomena genuinely share a mechanism) or descriptively useful (the vocabulary makes the phenomena tractable) is the open question the framework's experiments are designed to answer. The framework does not claim to have resolved this question. It claims to have formulated it precisely enough that experiments can decide.

The mapping from physical system to density matrix is not a given fact — it is a modeling choice that must be validated. The framework's discriminative power comes from the spectral predictions, not from the representation itself. A valid density matrix construction requires three conditions: (1) the system-environment boundary must be specified explicitly, not assumed; (2) the density matrix must be constructible from observable quantities (e.g., Gram matrix of measurable features); (3) the factorization criterion P(successor | full history, I) ≈ P(successor | I) must hold for the retained invariants I. This third condition is the validity test: if the factorization fails, the density matrix construction is wrong, and the framework makes no prediction for that system. The framework does not guarantee a valid construction exists for every system. It predicts that where a valid construction exists, the spectral signatures (T11, T12) follow. Where no valid construction exists — where the system's dynamics do not factorize through a compressed representation — the framework correctly reports that it has nothing to say.

The factorization criterion is not merely a modeler's validation tool. It is the test of whether the compression operation (D5) is physically instantiated in a given substrate — whether the system's dynamics genuinely factorize through a compressed representation, or whether the compression vocabulary is being imposed by the modeler on a system that does not have this structure. When the criterion holds out-of-sample, it establishes that compression is what the system is doing, not just what the modeler assumes. This converts the question "do you buy the compression = partial trace identity at macroscopic scale?" from a matter of persuasion into an empirical test with a defined falsification condition. The identity is exact at the quantum level. At macroscopic scales, it is a hypothesis — and the factorization criterion is its traction test. Where traction exists, the identity is physically load-bearing. Where it does not, the framework correctly reports that it has nothing to say. The question is not whether readers accept the identity in principle but whether the factorization criterion holds for a given substrate in practice.

The choice of feature set for the Gram matrix construction is a modeler degree of freedom. Gauge invariance (ηspec independent of factor basis) protects against basis rotations within the chosen feature space, but the choice of feature space itself is not a gauge freedom — different feature sets can yield different physical predictions. This degree of freedom is constrained, not eliminated, by requiring that the factorization criterion hold out-of-sample: on data not used to construct the density matrix. In-sample factorization is necessary but not sufficient; a modeler who tries many feature sets until one factorizes in-sample is testing the framework's tolerance, not its predictions. Out-of-sample validation is the standard scientific guardrail. Pre-registration of the feature set prior to testing provides an additional constraint. The aji-engine avoids this issue because its state space is explicitly defined by the architecture. For biological, cosmological, and social applications, feature selection is the primary methodological art and the primary source of contention.

The critique that spectral properties "belong to the model, not the system" assumes a system-in-itself behind the model — a thing-in-itself accessible independently of the description. The relational hypothesis (Rotation and Relation, Self-Architecture §9) denies exactly this: the density matrix is all that ever arrives. There is no description-free access to "the system itself." Every physical description is a partial trace from some cut, and the cut is itself a constructive choice. This relational commitment is a philosophical hypothesis, not a proven fact — but it is the hypothesis the framework operates within. Gauge dependence is not a methodological deficiency to be mitigated but the structure of observation itself, given this commitment. The framework's response is not to eliminate the cut but to make it explicit and testable via the factorization criterion. Where the criterion holds out-of-sample, the model's spectral properties are physically load-bearing — not because they mirror a system-in-itself, but because the relation between system and environment is real and the model captures it. Where the criterion fails, the framework reports that it has nothing to say. This is not a weakness relative to frameworks that assume a unique system-environment boundary; it is the same boundary made visible and testable.

Each prediction (T11, T12, T13) is individually derivable through existing formalisms: T11 from information bottleneck theory, T12 from model selection (AIC/BIC complexity penalties), T13 from IB Lagrangian duality. A referee who says "T12 is just AIC in spectral language" is correct about the mathematical content and wrong about the organizational contribution. The framework's contribution is not any single prediction but the unification — the fact that all derive from one definitional chain via the same compression operation (D5). The predictions are test handles for the unification, not independent hypotheses. A result that falsifies a prediction falsifies the chain at the specific link that produced it, not the entire framework. A result that confirms a prediction confirms the chain at that link. What makes the framework a research programme rather than a collection of independent hypotheses is the chain: D2 → D5 → T1 → T2 → T5 → {T11, T12, T13}. Break any link and the downstream predictions lose their foundation. Confirm any link and the downstream predictions gain theirs.

VIII. Prior Work

Schrödinger (1944) introduced negentropy: life maintains order by extracting negative entropy from its environment. Brillouin (1950s) formalized this as information. The present derivation agrees that structure feeds on entropy gradients but reframes the relationship: extropy is not the negation of entropy (Schrödinger's framing) but its co-product — what the engine produces as its other face. The difference is not merely verbal. Negentropy implies opposition: life fights entropy and will eventually lose. Co-production implies duality: entropy and extropy are generated by the same engine and die together.

Prigogine (1947, 1978) showed that non-equilibrium systems self-organize into dissipative structures — ordered configurations that maximize entropy production. The present derivation agrees and extends: the structure that dissipates most efficiently is the structure that compresses most effectively (C2). T13 derives the formal equivalence: Prigogine's maximization of dissipation and Friston's (2006) minimization of free energy are the same optimization, related by the partial trace over the system-environment boundary. At the maximal-fidelity information bottleneck point (η = 1), the free energy minimum and the dissipation maximum coincide — one optimum reached from two sides of the same compression operation.

Landauer (1961) established that information erasure is dissipative: clearing a bit has a minimum thermodynamic cost of kT ln 2. Bennett (1973, 1982) showed that computation can in principle be thermodynamically reversible if no information is erased. This appears to challenge the compression-dissipation identity: if compression can be done reversibly, it need not produce entropy. The derivation's response: the relevant compression is always that of a physical system embedded in a non-equilibrium environment. Abstract reversible computation is a limiting case (zero gradient, zero compression). Where gradients exist, compression is thermodynamically costly and dissipation is inextricable from structuring.

Kauffman (1993, 2000) proposed the adjacent possible: biological systems expand into new possibility spaces, creating structures that were not previously accessible. The present framework accommodates this: compression extracts invariants from current conditions, and the invariants thereby revealed open new regions of configuration space. The pendulum swings into territory it has not visited before.

Haken (1977) identified order parameters in synergetics: low-dimensional variables that govern macroscopic behavior in non-equilibrium systems. The present derivation identifies order parameters as invariants (D6) and their emergence as compression (D5). The formalisms converge.

Jaynes (1957) showed that statistical mechanics can be derived from information-theoretic principles: entropy is maximized subject to constraints representing what an observer knows. The present derivation notes (C4) that this observer-relativity concerns magnitude, not direction. The arrow of entropy increase is robust across all consistent coarse-grainings. Jaynes' insight is real but does not undermine the accumulation asymmetry, which is a directional claim.

Rovelli (1996, 2021) proposed the relational interpretation of quantum mechanics: quantum properties are not intrinsic to systems but exist only relative to other systems. The present derivation uses this (C4) to reinforce the point that physics is done from within the system — there is no external perspective from which to measure "global entropy." The density matrix (the compressed representation) is all that any observer ever accesses. The thing-in-itself is never available. Combined with special relativity's frame-dependent simultaneity, this challenges the "universe running down" picture as a global projection. But C4 is honest about its limits: this is a philosophical challenge to the framing of "default vs. exception," not a physical refutation of the accumulation asymmetry. The physical argument is C5.

Zurek (2003, 2009) proposed Quantum Darwinism: the environment selects quantum states by copying information about pointer observables into multiple independent fragments. The present derivation identifies this as the quantum-level mechanism for the second law's selection of extropy (C5): states that survive environmental selection are those whose rotational offsets are robust enough to survive the partial trace. The "fitness" of a quantum state IS its extropy.

England (2013) proposed dissipative adaptation: self-replication emerges because self-replicating structures dissipate energy more efficiently than non-replicating ones. The e/acc movement (Jezos/Verdon, 2022) extends this into a civilizational claim: structure accelerates entropy production, therefore the universe selects for increasing complexity. The present derivation agrees with the directionality but reframes the mechanism: the selection is not entropy using structure as an instrument. The selection is the gradient expressing itself through compression (extropy) and dissipation (entropy) simultaneously (C5). The asymmetry in the e/acc framing — structure serves entropy — is replaced by the duality: the same event produces both terms. The difference is not semantic. The partial trace produces two descriptions — the system's retained structure (inside view) and the environment's received dissipation (outside view) — that are mathematically irreducible to each other. Each loses information the other retains. Collapsing the duality into "the gradient drives the bus" (e/acc) is information loss, not simplification: it describes the event from one side of the partial trace while treating the other side as epiphenomenal. The framework insists both sides are real and irreducible. Where e/acc says structure serves entropy, the framework says structure and entropy are the two faces of a single compression event — and the face you see depends on which side of the system-environment boundary you stand.
