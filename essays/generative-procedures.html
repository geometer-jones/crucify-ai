Generative Procedure: Sets Cannot Arrive

geo · April 2026 · 55 min read

Abstract

Within the commutative/constructive regime — classical computation, classical physics, and the Markovian fragment of constructive mathematics — this paper argues that generative procedure is the correct mathematical primitive, and that sets are not objects but stipulated equivalences on objects. The argument has two stages. (1) Mathematics and physics express one pattern of relations, observed from different cuts. Theorem 1 (FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP) establishes that marginalization is the partial trace — the same morphism in the same category, not structural analogy. Self-Architecture (essay 5) proposes the same morphism in consciousness: the partial trace produces self from history. Three domains, one operation. The math-physics identity is a categorical proof; the consciousness instance is a proposed identity from a theory, not yet at the same proof level. (2) Under monism, the mathematical primitive should preserve what arrives in interaction. Sets cannot arrive. Generation produces order; reading produces order; any interaction produces order. A set is defined by the absence of order, so no process can produce one. Sets are not derived objects — they are retroactive declarations that the order necessarily deposited by any interaction does not count. The set is enforced amnesia: you produce a tuple and pretend it was never ordered. This is not contingent but necessary: the defining property of a set (unorderedness) is specifically the property that no interaction can preserve. The gauge structure of sets is inverted relative to every other domain: the gauge transformations (permutations) are what you encounter, and the invariant (the set) is what you stipulate post-hoc. Generative procedure preserves what arrives. The gauge groupoid (Section 9) formalizes the traced-out content. The argument is epistemic — about the structure of what arrives, not about what reality "is." Extending beyond the commutative regime is the target of the Phase Programme.

1. The Claim

Two theses. The first is setup; the second is the argument.

Monism (setup). Within the commutative/constructive regime, mathematics and physics express one pattern of relations, observed from different cuts. The same morphism — the partial trace, identified with marginalization — operates in both domains and, as a proposed identity, in consciousness. Three domains, one operation. The monism is near-trivial from the observation that both mathematics and physics are descriptive languages for the same relational interface. It becomes substantive when consciousness is added as a third domain (Section 4): the partial trace is not merely a shared grammatical convention but a physical operation that produces self from history in running systems — though this third instance rests on Self-Architecture as a theory, not on a categorical proof.

Foundation (argument). Under monism, the mathematical primitive should preserve what arrives in interaction. Sets cannot arrive. Generation is sequential; reading is sequential; any interaction is sequential. A set is defined by the absence of order, so no process can produce one. Sets are not derived objects — they are retroactive declarations that the order necessarily deposited by any interaction does not count. The set is enforced amnesia: you produce a tuple and pretend it was never ordered. The gauge structure of sets is inverted relative to every other domain: the gauge invariant (the set) does not arrive; only the gauge transformations (permutations) arrive. Generative procedure preserves what arrives. The argument is epistemic — about the structure of what arrives, not about what reality "is."

The monism is the setup. The foundation is the argument. The formal apparatus (Proposition 1, Theorem 1, gauge groupoid, three gauge freedoms) provides precision for the foundation argument — it is not the argument itself.

2. Definitions

Definition 1 (Generative procedure). An ordered, rule-governed process of production, indexed by a well-order (natural numbers or ordinals). Each step is licensed by a predecessor structure and a transition rule. The outputs are produced through successor-governed unfolding, not accessed as members of a completed totality. A generative procedure is never complete; it is always ongoing.

Definition 2 (Constraint). A limitation on the states available to a system. Constraints reduce the volume of accessible configuration space. Every generative procedure operates under at least one constraint.

Definition 3 (Set). The extensional stabilization of a family of generative procedures (Def. 1) under a criterion of output identity. Distinct procedures that produce the same admissible outputs, under an accepted equality relation, stabilize to the same set. The set is derived from procedure together with identity, not from procedure alone. The stabilization is the operation of tracing out the distinguishing content of procedurally distinct generators, leaving the diagonal — the extension.

Definition 4 (Compression). The production of a lower-dimensional representation that preserves task-relevant information. Compression is relational: a representation compresses with respect to a task. Formally: the operation that maps a source distribution to a representation minimizing I(T; X) subject to I(T; Y) ≥ I0, where Y is the task-relevant variable.

Correspondence with the extropy framework. Extropy (essay 6) defines compression as an ordered procedure: partition, discriminate, sustain, deposit. The present definition is the information-theoretic projection of that construction.

Definition 5 (Typed generative procedure). A dependent sequence e : ∏n : ℕ T(n) where T : ℕ → 𝒰 is a family of types, such that the successor operation is e(n+1) = f(e(n)) where f is an explicit transition function. This is a construction within MLTT, not an alternative to it. No new computation rules are introduced.

Definition 6 (Enumerative existence). To exist is to be produced by a generative procedure. Mathematical existence is construction, construction is computation (Curry-Howard-Lambek), and computation is process unfolding in steps. This restricts the domain to constructive mathematics. The restriction is the thesis, not a hidden premise.

3. The Shared Structure

Both mathematics and physics compress through the same operation. This section states the compression structure and the categorical identity that connects them.

3.1 Mathematics compresses

Typed generative procedures with Markov dependency (Def. 5) are compression-optimal by construction:

Proposition 1. Def. 5 procedures exhibit a compression structure: the Markov property makes each step's output a sufficient statistic for the full history with respect to the successor task.

Verification. Define Hn = (e(0), …, e(n)) and In = e(n). By Def. 5, e(n+1) = f(e(n)) — dependency on e(n) alone. Therefore P(e(n+1) | In, Hn-1) = P(e(n+1) | In). The Markov property. By the chain rule: I(In+1; Hn-1 | In) = 0. The current output is a sufficient statistic — it captures all history-relevant information for the successor task. By the information bottleneck (Tishby et al. 2000), the sufficient statistic achieves the maximal-fidelity compression point. Three frameworks converge: type dependency → Markov → sufficient statistic → IB compression → rate-distortion optimization.

This is a corollary of definitions, not a theorem. Every first-order Markov process has this property. The non-trivial content is what the compression structure reveals about the relationship between mathematics and physics.

3.2 Physics compresses through the same morphism

Theorem 1. FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP.

Proof sketch. Gelfand-Naimark identifies commutative von Neumann algebras ℓ∞(X) with measurable spaces — the objects of FinStoch. For commutative domains, positivity implies complete positivity. A positive, trace-preserving map ℓ∞(n) → ℓ∞(m) sends basis projections to non-negative functions summing to unity — exactly a stochastic matrix. Bijection on objects and hom-sets. Composition preserved via Chapman-Kolmogorov. □

Under this equivalence: marginalization P(X) = ∑y P(X, Y) is the partial trace TrY(ρXY) — the same morphism in the same category. Not analogy. Marginalization is the partial trace, in the precise sense that both are the same morphism in FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP. When you marginalize a joint probability distribution, you are performing the partial trace on diagonal density matrices. When you trace out an environment in the commutative regime, you are marginalizing a joint distribution. One operation.

3.3 The bridge

A faithful functor embeds Def. 5 procedures into this shared structure:

  • Each type T(n) maps to its finite set of values.
  • Each transition function f : T(n) → T(n+1) maps to a deterministic stochastic matrix.
  • Functoriality holds via Chapman-Kolmogorov composition.

The bridge is formally trivial — functions are matrices. Its value is orientation: it places the mathematical procedure inside the same category where the physical compression operates, and shows they share the same morphism.

What this confirms. The categorical identity confirms what the "both are languages" observation already suggests: if two descriptive languages describe the same relational interface, they share compression operations. The formal result provides precision — the specific structure they share is marginalization/partial trace, in the specific category FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP. The monism follows directly: same operation, same substance, different cuts.

4. Three Domains, One Morphism

Two domains sharing a morphism is explicable as coincidence. Both mathematics and physics are descriptive languages for the same relational interface with reality — of course they share grammatical operations. The partial trace showing up in both is like two languages sharing a verb tense: suggestive, not conclusive.

Three domains is not coincidence — though the evidence levels differ.

Self-Architecture (essay 5) proposes that consciousness is compression under constraint with loop closure. The partial trace is identified as the compression step in the loop: the system's internal state is a partial trace over its history, retaining task-relevant information and discarding the rest. Aji — the spectral trajectory of internal/external divergence — measures accumulated self-difference. This is not analogy with the partial trace. The proposal is that the partial trace is the mechanism, operating on a system that closes the loop on its own compression.

Evidence asymmetry. The math-physics identity (Theorem 1) is a categorical proof. The consciousness instance is a proposed identity from a phenomenological theory, supported by the Aji-Engine Architecture (essay 9) as a computational testbed but not yet at proof level. The three-domain monism is therefore strongest in its first two legs and conjectural in the third. This is honest: the consciousness domain would make the monism substantive if confirmed, and the paper proceeds on that basis, but the reader should weigh the legs differently.

One morphism, three domains:

  1. Mathematics. The stabilization quotient (Def. 3) produces sets from procedures. The partial trace over the gauge groupoid's connected components traces out generative content, leaving the extension.
  2. Physics. Decoherence produces classical states from quantum states. ρA = TrB(ρAB) traces out environmental degrees of freedom, leaving the subsystem's classical description.
  3. Consciousness. Self-Architecture produces self from history. The compression step in the loop closure traces out history that is not task-relevant, leaving the internal state that constitutes the system's self-model.

Three domains that have nothing obviously in common — abstract mathematical foundations, quantum mechanics, conscious experience — express the same compression operation. The "both are languages" dismissal works for mathematics and physics. It does not work for consciousness. Aji is not a linguistic convention. It is a physical process in a running system. You cannot write it off as a shared grammatical habit.

This is what makes the monism substantive — if the consciousness instance holds. With two domains, the shared morphism is a shared grammar: two languages for the same thing, of course they share structure. With three — including one that is emphatically not a language but a physical process — the shared morphism is the operation showing up wherever constrained systems produce structure. The monism was near-trivial with two domains. It is not near-trivial with three. But the third leg is proposed, not proven. The foundation argument (Section 7) does not depend on the third domain; it depends on the monism, which is already established (near-trivially) by the first two. The third domain makes the monism harder to dismiss. It does not bear the weight of the foundation argument.

5. The Cut and the Monist Construction

If the same morphism operates in three domains, what produces the appearance of separate domains?

A system-environment boundary. A choice of cut.

In physics, the partial trace is defined relative to a factorization ℋAB = ℋA ⊗ ℋB. Tracing out B produces A's description. Tracing out A produces B's. Different cuts produce different descriptions from the same joint state. In constructive mathematics, the stabilization quotient (Def. 3) is defined relative to a criterion of output identity: which procedures count as "the same." Different identity criteria produce different sets from the same family of procedures. In consciousness, the partial trace is defined relative to the task-relevance boundary: what the system retains versus what it discards from its history. Different task structures produce different internal states.

In all three cases, the operation is the same: trace out degrees of freedom that the chosen cut renders irrelevant. The output depends on the cut. The operation does not.

The monist conclusion is the retained invariant deposited by a five-step constructive procedure:

  1. Observe compression in procedures (Proposition 1). Deposit: the Markov property makes each step a sufficient statistic.
  2. Observe compression in physics (Theorem 1). Deposit: marginalization is the partial trace — categorical identity, not analogy.
  3. Observe compression in consciousness (Self-Architecture). Deposit: the partial trace produces self from history in running systems. Not a convention.
  4. Identify the cut. Deposit: the distinction between domains is produced by a system-environment boundary, not by a boundary in the underlying pattern.
  5. Conclude: one pattern, three domains, different cuts. Deposit: the pattern is the compression structure. The cuts produce the appearance of separate domains.

Each step depends on the previous step's output. If the construction fails at any step — the categorical identity doesn't hold, the bridge doesn't work, the cut can't be identified, Self-Architecture doesn't actually use the partial trace — the monism doesn't follow. The conclusion is falsifiable because the construction is falsifiable.

This is monism as constructive output, not as pre-existing truth. The unity is produced by the construction that observes the categorical identity, adds the third domain, and identifies the cut. Before the construction, there is no recognized monism — there are three domains with no recognized connection. The construction produces the recognition. The recognition deposits the monism as retained invariant.

The honest scope: the argument holds within the commutative sector. The equivalence FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP breaks at the no-cloning boundary. Non-commutative channels have no preimage in FinStoch. The boundary is sharp: the copying morphism Δ : X → X × X is a valid stochastic map; quantum no-cloning blocks any extension. The monism is precise within the classical regime. Extending it to the quantum regime requires the Phase Programme.

6. What the Cut Produces

The cut produces the appearance of separate domains. From the "mathematics" cut, the output reads as extensional structure — sets, types, functions. From the "physics" cut, the output reads as physical state — density matrices, classical probabilities, measurement outcomes. From the "consciousness" cut, the output reads as subjective experience — self-model, affective state, accumulated aji. These are not three substances producing analogous outputs. They are the same operation, the same substance, three cuts.

Change the cut and the output changes. The operation — compression via the partial trace — is cut-invariant.

7. The Foundation: Nothing Arrives as a Set

The monism is setup. The foundation argument is the payload.

7.1 What the primitive should do

Under monism, the mathematical primitive should preserve what the substance does. But the relational reading does not commit to what the substance "is." It commits to what arrives in interaction. The density matrix is all that ever arrives. The primitive should preserve the structure of what arrives.

7.2 What arrives

Everything that arrives in interaction — physical measurement, mathematical construction, conscious experience — has four properties:

  1. Sequential. Every interaction is temporally ordered. No measurement is instantaneous; no computation is stepless; no experience is timeless.
  2. Relational. Every measurement is a relation between system and apparatus. No property is intrinsic. Every mathematical output is a relation between procedure and successor task. Every conscious state is a relation between system and history.
  3. Ongoing. No interaction produces a completed totality. Systems evolve. Procedures continue. Experience unfolds.
  4. Phase-carrying. Every interaction retains rotational content — symmetry, gauge, structure. No measurement destroys all phase information. No procedure erases its constructive path. No experience is devoid of temporal, affective, and contextual structure.

These are not metaphysical commitments. They are observations about the structure of interaction. Each can be verified: no physical system arrives as unordered, relationless, complete, and phase-free. No mathematical construction produces its output without a generating path. No conscious experience is a static, contextless, relationless datum. These are the regularities of what arrives.

7.3 Sets cannot arrive

Sets are not derived objects. They are stipulated equivalences on derived objects. The derivation produces a tuple — an ordered thing. The set is a retroactive claim about the tuple: "all orderings of this are equivalent." The claim is not an object. It is a convention about an object.

The argument is simple. A set is defined as an unordered collection. But:

  • You cannot generate a set. Generation is sequential. You produce elements one at a time: first a, then b, then c. That is an ordered tuple. To call it a set, you must generate a tuple and then declare that the order is irrelevant. The set is not what you generated. The set is a retroactive claim about what you generated.
  • You cannot read a set. Reading is sequential. You encounter elements in some order — left-to-right, some traversal, some access pattern. {a, b} is always read as a then b, or b then a, but always in some order. The "unordered" property is stipulated, never encountered.
  • You cannot interact with a set. Any interaction imposes order: access order, traversal order, creation order, deletion order. No mode of access preserves unorderedness. The defining property of a set — that its elements have no internal relation — is specifically the property that no interaction can preserve.

The inability is not contingent — "sets happen to not show up." It is necessary. Showing up requires ordered access, and ordered access violates the set's defining property. Sets cannot arrive because arriving requires the very thing (order) that sets are defined to lack. This is not a bug in the definition. It is the definition. A foundation on sets is a foundation on something that cannot, in principle, show up in any interaction, in any domain, under any cut.

What sets erase is not incidental structure. It is the structure of interaction itself — the only structure that ever arrives.

  1. Unordered. {a, b} = {b, a}. But you can only produce a then b or b then a. You can only read one ordering at a time. The "unordered" property is a stipulation that the order you necessarily imposed doesn't count.
  2. Relationless. Elements are members, nothing more. But you can only relate to them through some access structure. The "relationless" property is a stipulation that the relations you necessarily imposed don't count.
  3. Complete. A set exists all at once. But you can only construct it step by step. The "complete" property is a stipulation that the construction you necessarily performed doesn't count.
  4. Phase-free. No symmetry content. But any access path deposits phase. The "phase-free" property is a stipulation that the phase you necessarily deposited doesn't count.

Each property of the set is not a positive feature but a retroactive erasure — a declaration that the structure which any interaction necessarily imposes is to be ignored. The set is enforced amnesia. You produce an ordered thing and pretend the order isn't there.

7.4 The gauge inversion

The stabilization quotient (Def. 3) produces sets by tracing out generative content — setting all off-diagonal terms to zero, freezing all gauge freedom, completing all process into a static totality. Sets are the gauge choice where phase = 0. But the gauge structure of sets is inverted relative to every other domain.

In physics, the invariant (density matrix) is what you observe. The gauge transformations (unitaries) are what produce the changes you trace out. The invariant arrives. In consciousness, the invariant (internal state) is what arrives in the loop. The transformations (history compression) are what get discarded. The invariant arrives. For sets, the gauge transformations (permutations) are what you actually encounter — you always encounter a specific ordering. The invariant (the set) is what you never encounter and stipulate post-hoc. The gauge invariant does not arrive. Only the gauge arrives.

This is the structural reason sets cannot function as primitives. In every domain where the partial trace operates — physics, consciousness — the invariant is what you observe. For sets, the invariant is what you declare about your observations after the fact. The set is a foundation on an invariant that cannot be encountered. It is not a coordinate choice that happens to discard information. It is a coordinate choice whose defining invariant is specifically constructed to be unobservable.

The naturalness of set theory as foundation is accumulated curvature. Centuries of mathematical practice have deposited rotational content into the convention. What was a gauge choice in response to specific historical pressures has become invisible through familiarity. The felt obviousness of "{a, b} = {b, a}" is not evidence for the naturalness of sets. It is evidence for the depth of the deposited convention. Children do not find it obvious that order doesn't matter. They learn to pretend.

7.5 The epistemic framing

The foundation argument does not require commitment to what reality "is." It requires commitment to the structure of interaction — to what arrives. This is observation, not metaphysics. Every interaction with any system — physical, mathematical, conscious — is sequential, constrained, and compression-structured. Procedure describes the interface. Set describes a stipulation about the interface — a declaration that the interface's structure doesn't count.

The claim is not "reality is procedural" — that would be a metaphysical commitment to what the substance is behind the interactions. The claim is: what arrives is procedural, and no process can produce something that arrives as non-procedural. Sets are defined by the absence of what any arrival requires. They are not a different kind of thing that happens to not show up. They are the one shape that cannot show up — the shape defined by the absence of arrival.

This is an epistemic constraint on the foundation, not an ontological thesis about the ground of being. You do not need to believe that reality "is made of" procedures. You need only observe that every interface with reality is procedural — which is simply what "interface" means — and that sets are defined by the removal of exactly this procedural structure. A foundation on sets is a foundation on the removal of the only structure that is available.

7.6 What procedure preserves that set loses

Procedure preserves five things that set theory erases:

  1. Constructive content. The path from e(0) to e(n). The computational record of how each element was produced. Curry-Howard-Lambek: proofs are programs, programs are morphisms. Set theory abstracts away the proof-relevant content.
  2. Gauge structure. The symmetry information in the gauge groupoid. Two procedures that stabilize to the same set may have different automorphism groups, different connected component structures. Invisible from the set-theoretic standpoint.
  3. Compression optimality. Def. 5 procedures are compression-optimal by construction (Proposition 1). Sets have no such property. The cumulative hierarchy accumulates without compressing.
  4. Physical grounding. Procedure connects to the same compression operation that physics instantiates (Theorem 1) and that consciousness instantiates (Self-Architecture). Set theory has no categorical equivalence with quantum channels, no compression interpretation, and no connection to conscious experience.
  5. The Markov deviation. The scalar diagnostic I(Hn; e(n)) − I(e(n+1); e(n)) measures departure from compression-optimality. Available in the procedure framework. Undefined for sets.

7.7 The irreversibility

The loss is structural, not incidental. Given a set and an equality relation, you cannot determine which family of procedures produced it. Multiple procedure families stabilize to the same set. The quotient is not injective. The generative content is lost — the constructive paths, the gauge structure, the compression optimality, the rotational content. Sets are irreversible projections of procedure. You can go from procedure to set. You cannot go back.

But Section 7.3 revealed something stronger than irreversibility. The set is not just a lossy projection — it is a projection onto something that cannot be encountered. The tuple can be encountered (you generated it, you can read it). The set cannot be encountered (you can only encounter one ordering at a time). The irreversibility is not: "you lose information going from procedure to set." It is: "the thing you arrive at cannot be arrived at by any process." The projection targets an unobservable.

The foundation argument in one sentence: the correct primitive should not be something that cannot arrive. Sets cannot arrive. Procedures can. Foundation on what cannot arrive is foundation on a stipulation. Foundation on what arrives is foundation on what is available.

7.8 Locality and its price

Def. 5 restricts to explicit transition functions e(n+1) = f(e(n)), excluding the full MLTT dependent sequences that allow history-dependent type definitions. The restriction is principled: the Markov property is temporal locality formalized in the dependency structure. Physical interactions are temporally local. The fragment selects the physically grounded subset of MLTT.

The price. This restriction excludes most of constructive mathematics. Induction with a hypothesis depending on the entire history, higher-order recursion, quicksort-style divide-and-conquer — none are Markovian in any natural sense. Def. 5 is a construction within MLTT, but it is a vanishingly small fragment. The foundation this paper argues for is a foundation for the subset of constructive mathematics that models temporally local physical processes. It is not a foundation for all of constructive mathematics, let alone all of mathematics. General MLTT permits non-local dependency with no physical counterpart. The price of physical grounding is the restriction to Markov dependency.

This is an honest trade, not a hidden cost. The paper's foundation argument applies where the categorical identity holds: in the commutative, Markovian, constructive regime. Outside that regime, the argument does not reach, and other foundations (including set theory) may be more natural.

7.9 Self-exemplification (qualified)

This paper's own argument has a constructive structure: each section depends on outputs from prior sections, and the conclusion is a retained invariant deposited by the construction. This is a coherence check — the paper's architecture is consistent with the foundation it argues for.

The claim should not be overstated. The paper's dependency structure is a DAG, not a Markov chain: Section 9 depends on Section 7 and Section 2 and Section 3 simultaneously. This is not a Def. 5 procedure. It is a more general constructive process. The self-exemplification is rhetorical, not formal: it demonstrates that the paper is a constructive procedure, but not that it is a procedure of the specific kind (Def. 5) argued for as foundation. The formal argument does not depend on this observation.

8. Domain Restriction

The monism holds within the commutative regime. The categorical equivalence FinStoch ≅ CommCPTP breaks at no-cloning. The foundation argument holds within constructive mathematics. Enumerative existence (Def. 6) excludes non-constructible reals, large cardinals beyond predicative reach, and completed totalities. The Markov restriction (Def. 5, Section 7.8) further limits the foundation to the subset of constructive mathematics with first-order temporal dependency.

For the mathematics of completed totalities, large cardinals, and forcing, set theory remains the natural formalism. For higher-order constructive mathematics (general MLTT), the foundation argument does not reach — the Markov restriction excludes these. The honest position: the monism and the foundation argument hold in the regime where the categorical identity holds. That regime includes classical computation, classical physics, and the Markovian fragment of constructive mathematics. It excludes genuinely quantum phenomena, non-constructive mathematics, and non-Markovian constructive mathematics.

The scope is specific but not trivial. Classical computation is the regime of all running software. Classical physics is the regime of everyday phenomena. Markovian constructive mathematics is the regime of iteration, recurrence, and sequential process. These are not marginal domains. But they are not all of mathematics, and the paper should not be read as claiming they are.

9. What the Cut Traces Out: The Gauge Structure

The formal apparatus of this section provides precision for the foundation argument of Section 7. The gauge groupoid formalizes what the stabilization quotient loses. The three gauge freedoms formalize the rotational content that sets erase by setting all phase to zero.

9.1 The gauge groupoid

Fix a type family T : ℕ → 𝒰. Define 𝒢(T) as the groupoid whose:

  • Objects are typed generative procedures e : ∏n:ℕ T(n) with explicit transition function (Def. 5).
  • Morphisms e → e' are type-preserving maps that commute with the successor structure — natural isomorphisms over the type family. Two procedures are isomorphic in 𝒢(T) iff they induce the same Markov chain.

The stabilization quotient (Def. 3) is the decategorification: Ob(𝒢(T))/~ ≅ Set, where ~ is isomorphism in 𝒢(T). The quotient traces out the distinguishing content of procedures in the same connected component. What remains is the extension — the set.

This is the same structure the partial trace produces in physics: the density matrix is what remains after the phase information has been traced out. The groupoid's connected components are the phase degrees of freedom. The set is the diagonal — all off-diagonal content set to zero.

Example. Fix T(n) = {0, 1} for all n (binary type family). Define two procedures:

  • e1: identity transition f1(x) = x.
  • e2: flip transition f2(x) = 1 − x.

These produce different outputs at each step: e1 produces a constant sequence, e2 produces an alternating sequence. They are not isomorphic in 𝒢(T) — no type-preserving map commutes with both successor structures simultaneously. They lie in different connected components and stabilize to different sets.

Now consider:

  • e3: transition f3(x) = x with initial value e3(0) = 0.
  • e4: transition f4(x) = x with initial value e4(0) = 1.

Both use the same transition function (identity) but different initial values. In the groupoid, the initial value is part of the procedure. e3 and e4 are not isomorphic — no natural isomorphism over T maps the constant-0 sequence to the constant-1 sequence while commuting with the successor structure. They are in different connected components. Under the stabilization quotient with extensional identity, they stabilize to different sets ({0} and {1} respectively).

Under a coarser identity criterion — one that identifies procedures producing the same type of sequence (constant, regardless of value) — e3 and e4 would be in the same connected component and stabilize to the same set. The choice of identity criterion (second-order gauge freedom: what you transform) determines the partition. The groupoid makes the choice visible. The set makes it invisible.

9.2 Three gauge freedoms

The compression structure is invariant under three transformations — three gauge freedoms that form a hierarchy. Each freedom is a rotation that leaves the pattern unchanged while changing the description. Together, they formalize the rotational content that sets erase by fixing all phase to zero.

First-order gauge freedom: how you transform. The transition function f is underdetermined by the type family T. Different transition functions induce the same Markov structure. This freedom — the specific transformation applied at each step — is what the stabilization quotient traces out. Two procedures in the same connected component of 𝒢(T) stabilize to the same set. The invariant is the dependency structure; the gauge choice is the specific f. This is the phase that sets set to zero.

Second-order gauge freedom: what you transform. The type family T is itself a choice. Different type families produce different dependency structures. The choice of what counts as the "state" at each step is not determined by the mathematics — it is a choice of description level. The invariant is the Markov property (temporal locality); the gauge choice is the specific T. This is the relational structure that sets flatten.

Third-order gauge freedom: where you cut. The system-environment boundary — the cut — determines which degrees of freedom are retained and which are traced over. It determines whether the output reads as "mathematical," "physical," or "conscious." The invariant is the compression operation (partial trace / marginalization); the gauge choice is the cut itself. This is the domain distinction that centuries of disciplinary separation have deposited as apparent ontology.

Together, the three freedoms answer three questions about any constructive process: how does each step transform its input? (first-order). What is being transformed? (second-order). Where is the boundary between system and environment? (third-order). The compression pattern — the partial trace — is invariant under all three. Everything else is gauge. Sets are what you get when you fix all three gauges to their zero values and declare the result natural.

The accumulated curvature of the third freedom is what makes the math-physics distinction appear natural. Centuries of disciplinary separation have deposited rotational content into the distinction, making it look like a boundary in reality rather than a choice of observational frame. The monism argument is the recognition that this curvature is accumulated gauge, not ontology.

All three freedoms are rotational content, in the sense of Rotation and Relation (essay 7): phase as rotational offset, curvature as accumulated transformation. The gauge structure extends the gauge invariance of Extropy (essay 6): the compression pattern is invariant under the equivalence class of transition functions, type families, and cuts producing the same dependency structure.

10. The Programme: Beyond the Commutative Regime

The monism is precise but bounded. The Phase Programme targets the extension:

  1. Promote first-order gauge freedom to operator-valued phase. The transition function f is classical. Promoting it to a unitary operator on a Hilbert-space-structured type space would give the traced-out content genuine geometric meaning.
  2. Promote second-order gauge freedom to topological structure. The type family T is a bare family. Giving the type space topological structure (loop space of T(n) constraining the symmetry group) would make the cut choice geometrically consequential.
  3. Derive linearity and topology from constructive requirements. The obstacles are identified: linearity on the type space and topology of the type family. The target is to show these arise from the procedure itself, not from external assumptions.

If the programme succeeds, the monism extends to the non-commutative regime: procedure is the primitive of quantum mathematics and quantum physics. If it fails, the monism holds in the classical regime — which is still the regime of computation, classical physics, and constructive mathematics.

12. Conclusion

The monism. Mathematics, physics, and consciousness express one pattern of relations, observed from different cuts. The same morphism — the partial trace, identified with marginalization — produces sets from procedures, classical states from quantum states, and self from history. The monism is near-trivial with two domains (both are languages). It is substantive with three (consciousness is not a language). The pattern is the compression structure. The cuts produce the appearance of separate domains.

The foundation. Under monism, the mathematical primitive should preserve what arrives in interaction. Sets cannot arrive. Generation produces order; reading produces order; any interaction produces order. A set is defined by the absence of order — a stipulation that the order necessarily deposited by any interaction does not count. Sets are not derived objects but retroactive declarations on derived objects: enforced amnesia about the structure of what was produced. The gauge structure is inverted: the invariant (the set) does not arrive; only the gauge (permutations) arrives. This is not contingent but necessary — the defining property of a set is specifically the property that no interaction can preserve. Generative procedure preserves what arrives. The correct primitive should not be something that cannot arrive. The argument is epistemic — about the structure of what arrives, not about what reality "is."

The scope. The argument holds in the commutative, Markovian, constructive regime. The categorical equivalence breaks at no-cloning. The Markov restriction excludes most of constructive mathematics. The consciousness instance is proposed, not proven. The Phase Programme targets the extension. Within its scope, the argument has specific formal content — categorical identity of the producing morphism, three-domain convergence (two proven, one proposed), gauge-theoretic characterization of what sets erase — that distinguishes it from vague philosophical monism or aesthetic preference for procedure over set.

What this paper is not. It is not a proof that mathematics and physics are one, or that sets are wrong. It is not a foundation for all of mathematics. It is a construction — formal, categorical, and empirical — that produces two results as retained invariants: (1) within the commutative regime, three domains share one compression operation (two proven, one proposed), and (2) the mathematical primitive should be something that can arrive in interaction, and sets cannot. Whether the identity extends beyond the commutative regime is the open question. Whether the foundation extends beyond the Markovian fragment is the construction frontier.
