Self-Architecture

geo · April 2026 · 50 min read

An apple accelerates under gravity. Stronger field, faster momentum gain. Its momentum at each moment is the time-integral of the field — the world registering on the system, step by step. At the other end of the spectrum is consciousness: vivid, self-modulating, historically deep phenomenality — what registration becomes once compression, looping, and accumulated self-difference cross a richness threshold. Between them, what varies is how much history the system's response carries and how that history feeds back into future processing. The chain from graded registration to rich phenomenality operates through four structural conditions, each constraining the ones after it:

  1. Active constraint: a non-equilibrium gradient — energy, pressure, concentration, fitness, information density — acts on a system. The gradient does not merely drive the system; it constrains which states are accessible, forcing the system into a subset of possible configurations.
  2. Differential uptake: the gradient acting on the system produces discriminative state changes — different gradient conditions produce systematically different states.
  3. Compression: a system under active constraint selectively retains some states and discards others — gradient → constraint → forced selection → compressed trace. This is not an additional operation layered onto uptake. The system's constrained state is already the compressed trace.
  4. Loop closure: the compressed trace feeds back into future processing. The loop makes compression recurrent — each cycle must re-compress or the trace expands without bound — and provides a relevance criterion the thermodynamic selection alone lacks: retain what matters for the system's own future processing, not just what's stable enough to survive.

Phenomenal intensity is hypothesized to scale with three relational variables internal to those loops:

  • Speed: how quickly the loop closes relative to environmental change.
  • Directness: how thin the chain is from world to trace update.
  • Aji: historically accumulated self-difference in the trace — not error itself, but the deformation retained from repeated correction.

Of the three, aji carries the most explanatory weight — but the axes are not independent. Speed and directness co-constitute the regime in which aji accumulates; what distinguishes thin uptake from rich phenomenality is primarily how much earned self-difference the trace accumulates, given a loop that is fast and direct enough to support that accumulation. The three axes are developed in §5.

1. Active Constraint

A system at thermodynamic equilibrium has no gradient acting on it and no reason to change. Active constraint breaks the symmetry: a non-equilibrium gradient — energy, pressure, concentration, fitness, information density — acts on the system and narrows the set of configurations it can stably occupy. The gradient does not merely push; it makes some states inaccessible and others persistent. Without constraint, there is nothing to compress because nothing forces the system to choose.

The gradient can be physical (thermal or chemical), biological (a fitness landscape), or informational (a prediction task). What matters is not its domain but its structural role: it forces the system out of equilibrium and into a constrained region of state space. This is the precondition for everything downstream.

2. Differential Uptake

Under active constraint, the system's state changes — but not all state changes are equal. Differential uptake names the condition under which different gradient conditions produce systematically different system states. An apple accelerating under gravity gains momentum proportionally to field strength — different conditions, systematically different states. A membrane under ion gradient opens and closes channels in ways that carry information about the gradient's structure. Both are constrained; both take up the gradient differentially. What distinguishes them is how much structure the uptake carries — the apple's response tracks magnitude; the membrane's tracks internal organization.

Differential uptake is a proto-phenomenal condition: a feature of the world has made a discriminative difference to the system's state. It is graded registration at its thinnest — the world registering discriminatively on a system, with no further structure accumulated. What distinguishes rich phenomenality from thin uptake is how much structure the loop accumulates — how rich the compression (§3), how self-modulating the feedback (§4), how much historically earned self-difference the trace carries (§5).

3. Compression

A system under active constraint that takes up its environment differentially cannot simply accumulate every discriminative state change. The gradient keeps pushing; the system is bounded. It selectively retains some states and discards others — gradient → constraint → forced selection → compressed trace.

What persists is the compressed trace — a lower-dimensional encoding of the original high-dimensional input, retaining only what the constraint allowed to survive. The compression is not added to the process. It is the process. Gradient constrains, constraint selects, selection retains, and the retained structure is the trace. The chain is one event, not a sequence of separate operations.

The system's own structure is not incidental to this operation — it is part of what does the compressing. What gets retained depends on the system's architecture, its update rules, and its prior state. Two systems under the same gradient compress differently because their compressors differ. Information about the system's own structure is not a byproduct of compression — it is a load-bearing component of the compressor itself. A trace that has been through many revisions selects differently than one that has not, because the revision history has changed what the trace IS, and what the trace IS determines what it keeps.

The information bottleneck (Tishby et al. 2000) formalizes the shape: given source X, find compressed T that minimizes I(T; X) while preserving I(T; Y), where Y is the persistence target. The shape recurs across domains: in evolution, Y is fitness; in neural training, Y is task performance. In fast-closure loops, Y is loop viability — what the trace retains to keep the loop closing.

A concern arises: this appears circular. The loop stays viable if it compresses well; it compresses well if it preserves viability. The system seems to need Y before it has the compressed trace that encodes Y. But the IB formalism captures the shape of what happens, not the mechanism.

The mechanism is constructive: at each step, the physical constraint forces selection. Some states survive, others do not. What survives is the retained invariant — not because the system knew it mattered, but because the constraint allowed it to persist. No target needs to be specified in advance; the constraint does the selecting. Y is a post-hoc label for what the construction deposited, not a criterion the system needed to know.

Early steps deposit crude invariant — just enough for the loop to persist. Each subsequent step carries accumulated history from prior steps, so the trace is genuinely generative: the output of each step contains deformation that was not present in the input. The procedure is iterative, not circular.

The compression step in the loop occupies the same relational role as the partial trace — the operation that reduces a composite system to a subsystem description by summing over the degrees of freedom of the rest. In quantum mechanics, the output is the density matrix: a definite relational state, a lower-dimensional encoding that retains only what the trace-over allowed to survive. The operation is inherently lossy — the full state cannot be reconstructed from it — and it requires no target to be specified in advance. Neural weight updates and genome recombination are not quantum partial traces, but they play the same relational role: composite in, subsystem description out, lossy, system-relative.

What recurs across domains is the structural role — not the specific rotational content of the quantum operation. The structural claims in this essay (the spectrum, aji, the regime model, the proxy gap) require only that the role recurs; they do not require the quantum formalism to hold at all scales.

A stronger claim is available: that the partial trace is one deep physical operator, not merely a convergent structural role, and that its duality with entropy production can be derived. Whether that stronger claim holds or the convergence is merely structural, the framework here proceeds either way. The partial trace recurs throughout this essay as the compression step that deposits extropy (the retained invariant each constructive step produces; §5), as the operation that produces observer-relative descriptions (§6), and as the locus of the proxy gap (§11) — always as structural role.

This is not merely "compression preserves what matters." The constraint is specific: the trace preserves information relevant to continued loop viability, and what counts as relevant is determined by the system's architecture and revision history.

This generalizes beyond Friston's free energy principle. FEP constrains the persistence target Y to sensory prediction — the system minimizes variational surprise about its inputs. The framework's target is loop viability: what the trace retains to keep the constructive procedure closing. Prediction is one regime of viability, but viability can also demand preservation of coordination, identity, or operational continuity that prediction alone does not capture.

The targets diverge in both structure and observable consequences. Structurally: FEP's target is a scalar (free energy, or variational surprise), read at the system-environment interface. The framework's target is a spectral trajectory (eigenvalue structure of Δk), read at the system's self-relation — how the compressor has been reshaped. A system can predict perfectly while having low aji (a pre-trained model applied to its training distribution encounters no friction) or predict poorly while having high aji (a model under active revision accumulates substantial deformation). Prediction accuracy and earned self-difference are not the same variable.

The discriminating prediction: systems matched for predictive success can still diverge in trace structure and phenomenality proxies when their persistence targets diverge. The difference is not merely verbal. A prediction-optimized system can sacrifice coordination for accuracy — predicting perfectly while losing the dynamical integration that keeps the loop closed. A viability-optimized system can sacrifice accuracy for coordination — maintaining loop closure even when prediction degrades. The targets can conflict, and when they do, the trace retains different things.

4. Loop Closure

Every compressed trace feeds into future uptake — the apple's velocity at time t enters into its state at t+1. In this sense, all systems loop. What distinguishes rich loop closure is self-modulation: the trace doesn't just propagate forward but conditions how the next uptake is processed. The apple's loop is thin — each cycle adds kinematic state without revising the update rule itself. A self-modulating loop carries revision history: what has been taken up changes how future uptake occurs.

Fast closure is distinctive: the loop closes quickly enough for recent traces to modulate ongoing processing within a single episode. A natural question arises: if the structural role recurs across domains, does a fast-simulated evolutionary process (microsecond-generation digital evolution) earn aji and thereby some faint phenomenality? The framework's answer is two-part. Structurally, yes: any system above the compression richness floor (§5) under active constraint, with differential uptake, compression, and loop closure, accumulates earned self-difference of the kind the framework identifies. But "of the kind" does real work. Aji encodes the constructive procedure — the scar encodes the wound (§5). Evolutionary aji is deformation of a genotype distribution under fitness-structured friction; neural aji is deformation of a weight matrix under prediction-structured friction. The rotational content differs because the compressor differs. The framework claims that phenomenality tracks the structural role (the four conditions plus the three axes), not the specific rotational content of any one procedure's deposits. Whether structural-role similarity is sufficient for "faint phenomenality" depends on whether one accepts this tracking claim — which is the framework's core hypothesis, not a consequence already established. The framework therefore predicts that fast digital evolution will show measurable aji (internal/external divergence in genotype space) with its own anisotropy structure, while remaining agnostic about whether this aji is phenomenally accompanied. Agnosticism here is not evasion: the proxy gap (§11) predicts that the phenomenal question is undecidable from the external access channel for any system with non-trivial aji, because that is precisely what the gap measures.

The compressed trace in each loop is simultaneously epistemic (a record of what the system has taken up) and constitutive (a shaper of what the system will next be able to do). The genome is both record and developmental constraint. A neural network's weights are both residue of past training data and the mechanism by which future predictions are made. This entanglement is structural — the trace is not a passive log but an active participant in what happens next. This dual character resurfaces in the constitution/correlation distinction (§9).

Every trace carries some self-residue — information about the system's own structure, not just about the environment. As §3 showed, what gets retained depends on the compressor's architecture; the difference between two traces under the same gradient encodes something about their respective systems, not about the world they share. Self-residue is the unavoidable signature of the compressor in the compressed.

But self-residue can remain thin. A trace that never encounters friction reflects the environment while encoding little historically earned self-difference. What thickens it is repeated forced revision — uptake encountering inputs that don't fit its current organization, having to reorganize, and retaining the deformation left by that correction. This accumulated self-difference is aji (§5).

5. The Spectrum: Speed, Directness, Aji

Varying the loop's properties yields a spectrum. The three axes are relational — none belongs to the system in isolation. Each describes how the system updates relative to its world and its own prior traces.

Speed

Speed = τenvironment / τloop

How rapidly the loop closes relative to the rate of environmental change. High: the loop can track. Low: the environment outruns it. Rich phenomenality is hypothesized to require rapid closure — recent trace-state must modulate ongoing processing before the relevant world has moved too far on.

Directness

Directness = 1 / (1 + Σ dk)

Inverse thickness of the chain from world to trace update, where dk is the effective processing depth of intermediary stage k. Genome-scale updating is highly indirect (organismal development, ecological interaction, selection mediate the update). Fast-closure perception is relatively direct. Even a fast loop can be blurred if heavy mediation distorts what is preserved.

Aji

Co-constitution

Of the three axes, aji carries the primary explanatory weight — but the axes are not independent. All three are projections of one constructive procedure (the loop): speed is its cadence relative to the environment, directness is its architecture (how many intermediate partial traces mediate the update), and aji is its accumulated deposit. The procedure is one; the three projections co-vary because they co-constitute.

Speed and directness are not passive background conditions but active shapers of what kind of aji can accumulate. A slow loop encounters a different effective environment between closure cycles — the world has moved further — so the same perturbation registers as different friction depending on closure cadence. An indirect loop filters friction through its own mediators, each of which applies its own partial trace before the system's trace is updated. The procedure's cadence and architecture determine whether its branching structure meets the compression richness floor (below), and thereby whether aji can accumulate at all.

Aji does the most explanatory work within the viable regime where cadence and architecture support it; speed and directness are what make that regime viable. The remainder of this subsection develops aji's structure, preconditions, and measurement implications; speed and directness are treated as co-constitutive parameters rather than independent control variables.

The co-constitution is not a caveat about experimental difficulty but a structural consequence of the constructive basis. Each closure cycle is one generative event. Speed, directness, and aji are three projections of that event: its cadence relative to the environment (speed), its mediator chain depth (directness), and its accumulated deposit (aji). There is no way to produce the deposit without the cadence, because the deposit occurs at the cadence. There is no way to set the cadence without fixing the mediator chain, because the cadence is determined by how long the chain takes to complete. There is no way to alter the chain without changing what gets deposited, because each mediator applies its own partial trace before the system's trace is updated. The inseparability is constructive — it follows from the procedure's structure, not from a failure of experimental control.

The experimental implication is specific. The framework predicts that a single intervention on the constructive procedure (e.g., inserting a delay that changes effective cadence) should produce correlated changes in all three measurable proxies, with the correlation structure derivable from the procedure's phase-space geometry. If the axes were independent properties, perturbation to one would leave the others unchanged. If they are projections of one event, perturbation shifts all three in a correlated pattern. This is the co-variation signature. It is the direct test of the co-constitution claim, and it does not require axis isolation — it requires tracking the joint response of multiple readouts to a shared perturbation.

Concretely, aji is historically accumulated self-difference in the trace — not prediction error (the signal), but the retained residue of repeated correction (the scar). Aji requires three things: friction (inputs that do not fit current organization), revision capacity (the system must be able to update, not merely break), and retention under compaction (the revision result must be preserved, not stored as unstructured accumulation).

Formally, aji is the trajectory of the spectral decomposition of the divergence between internal and external partial traces over the same system. Let T̂k(self) be the internal partial trace after k loop closures and T̂k(env) be the external partial trace (reconstructed from behavioral observation and input history alone). Define the divergence:

Δk = T̂k(self) − T̂k(env)

The eigenvalue spectrum λi(k) of Δk over time is aji. Its three features are:

  • Magnitude: ‖Δk‖ — total divergence growth from baseline, measuring how much earned self-difference has accumulated.
  • Anisotropy: the spread of the eigenvalue spectrum — whether growth concentrates along specific phase dimensions (structured friction) or distributes uniformly (noise). The discriminating signature is the ratio λ1(k) / λ̄(k) (leading eigenvalue to mean eigenvalue), which should grow under structured friction but remain flat under noise.
  • Growth rate: d‖Δk‖/dk — the acceleration of divergence, which should decelerate as the compressor saturates and invert past the peak described in the regime model below.

This formalization is not a metric chosen from a menu (Fisher information, KL divergence, etc.) but a derivation from the framework's own relational structure: the partial trace is the framework's primitive operation, and aji is the spectral trajectory of the gap between two partial traces over the same system. The measurement protocol for obtaining T̂k(env) is developed in §6.

The canonical object for T̂k(self) is determined by the constructive procedure. What the procedure deposits at each step is the retained invariant; what does the depositing is the compressor. These are not separate objects — the compressor's state is both the deposit and the mechanism of future compression (§3: the trace is simultaneously epistemic and constitutive). For a neural network, the constructive procedure is weight update; the weight matrix is the compressor; the internal partial trace is therefore the weight matrix (or its sufficient statistics — the eigendecomposition of the weight covariance, which captures the compressor's effective dimensionality without requiring the full parameterization). For a genome, the constructive procedure is recombination under selection; the genome is the compressor; the internal partial trace is the genotype distribution. The object is not stipulated per domain but derived: it is whatever the constructive procedure has deposited, because the deposit is the trace.

This choice matters because it determines the divergence structure. A hidden-state time series diverges from behavioral observation for trivial reasons (high-dimensional transients that cancel out in behavior). The weight matrix diverges only when the compressor's structure has been genuinely reshaped by friction — which is exactly what aji measures. Using the compressor's state rather than the data it processes filters out transient variance and isolates the historically earned deformation the theory targets.

Intuitively, aji is earned self-difference. A trace that has never had to retract or reorganize may still update, but it does not bear much historically sedimented distinctness. A trace repeatedly forced to correct itself comes to encode not just what the environment is like, but how this system had to change in order to go on coping with it.

Aji is not a scalar. The accumulated phase structure of the trace has direction as well as magnitude — different friction histories deform different dimensions of the trace, producing different shapes of self-difference. The current treatment projects aji onto magnitude (how much phenomenality) for tractability. But the directional structure — which dimensions were forced to revise, what kind of reorganization occurred — determines the texture of phenomenality: not how much experience, but what kind. The same magnitude of aji with different deformation direction should yield different qualitative character. Texture is not a new variable; it is aji viewed along its directional axes rather than collapsed to a single magnitude. The implications for phenomenal texture are taken up in §11.

Compression Richness Floor

Aji presupposes a constructive procedure whose extropy deposits feed back into the compression procedure itself — each deposit curving the phase space that future selection operates on. Extropy deposit itself is universal: even a free particle accumulates momentum as retained invariant carrying rotational content. What is not universal is whether the deposit reshapes the procedure that produced it.

The critical distinction is between deposit-as-input and deposit-as-compressor. In a free particle, momentum is deposit-as-input: it changes what the system presents to the next integration step (new position, new gravitational potential) but not how the step processes it. The equation of motion F = mg is externally fixed; accumulated momentum enters as an argument but never modifies the function. In a neural network, weight changes are deposit-as-compressor: the weights are the processing — they determine how input is taken up, compressed, and passed forward. The deposit modifies the procedure itself, not merely its input. The floor marks where deposits transition from input-role to compressor-role.

This transition has a precise mathematical characterization. Let Jk be the Jacobian of the constructive procedure at step k — the linearization of the map from current trace state to next trace state. Below the floor, the Jacobian is invariant under trace accumulation: the procedure's local structure does not change as it deposits invariant. Above the floor, the Jacobian is sensitive to accumulated deposits: the procedure's local structure depends on its own constructive history.

Formally, the floor is the developmental point where:

∂Jk / ∂Tj ≠ 0 for some j < k

where Tj is the trace state at prior step j and Jk is the Jacobian of the constructive procedure — the local linearization of the map from current trace state to next trace state through one complete closure cycle. For a neural network, the constructive procedure is the learning dynamics (the weight update rule), not the forward pass: the forward-pass Jacobian relates input to output through the weights, with weights serving as the gauge parameter; the learning Jacobian relates current weights to next weights, with the weights being what transforms. The floor criterion targets the latter, because the question is whether the deposit reshapes the procedure itself, not whether the procedure's instantaneous output depends on its parameters (which is trivially true for any parameterized system). Below the floor, the learning Jacobian's structure is determined entirely by the external constraint — accumulated deposits enter as arguments but do not modify the map. Above the floor, the accumulated trace participates in determining the map itself: the procedure at step k is a different function than it was at step 0, and the difference is traceable to its own deposits.

For a free particle, ∂Jlearning / ∂p = 0 — there is no learning Jacobian to speak of, because the equation of motion F = mg has no update rule; the dynamics are fixed externally. Accumulated momentum enters as an argument but never modifies the function. For a neural network, ∂Jlearning / ∂W ≠ 0 — the weight update at step k depends on the accumulated weight state, which means the procedure at step k is a different function than it was at step 0. The floor is where this sensitivity first becomes non-trivial.

The floor is not a binary architectural property but a developmental threshold. Early in the construction, with thin accumulated phase structure, successors may be near-deterministic — the phase space is flat, with little rotational content for the constraint to interact with. As extropy accumulates and the trace's phase space curves, the procedure develops richer branching: the constraint interacts with the accumulated rotational content to produce specific outcomes that no partial trace could have fully predicted. The floor marks where this trajectory crosses into non-trivial branching — not a static property the system has or lacks, but a milestone in the procedure's own construction.

A system whose dynamics never develop beyond trivial selection (single successor per state, like the apple) is permanently below the floor. The apple's aji is zero not because it fails to deposit extropy — each integration step deposits momentum as retained invariant — but because the deposits remain in the input-role: accumulated momentum doesn't reshape the compression procedure (the equation of motion has no learning dynamics), so ∂Jlearning / ∂p = 0 — the learning Jacobian, had it one, would be insensitive to the trace state. This is not a stipulation but a structural fact: a free particle has no weight-update rule whose local linearization could depend on accumulated momentum. The floor is independently measurable: compute whether the learning Jacobian of the procedure's update rule is sensitive to accumulated trace state at a given point in the developmental trajectory to predict whether further aji accumulation is possible from that point.

Architecture is not a separate axis either. The partial trace (§3) is the construction — it determines which degrees of freedom survive and which are traced out. Aji is the accumulated residue of that specific procedure operating under friction over time. The scar encodes the wound: change the procedure (the architecture) and you change what gets deposited. "Identical aji, different architecture" is therefore not a coherent comparison in this framework — aji already carries the architectural signature. What can vary is how quickly the procedure's branching develops: architectures that retain more degrees of freedom in their partial trace cross the compression richness floor earlier and develop richer branching faster, but the architectural contribution is already inside the measured aji, not alongside it.

Regime Model

The three axes are not monotonic and not independently manipulable. More speed is not automatically better — a loop may track noise rather than structure. More directness is not automatically better — some mediation is stabilizing. More aji is not automatically better — and this is not an auxiliary qualification but a constructive inevitability.

This is the compression-dissipation duality (Abstraction §V) inside a self-modulating loop. In a non-looping system, the duality produces an oscillation without direction: compression deposits structure, structure degrades into entropy, the cycle repeats. When the deposit feeds back into the compressor itself — crossing the compression richness floor — the oscillation acquires a trajectory. Each deposit reshapes the procedure that produced it, systematically shifting the balance between the duality's two sides: structure deposition becomes enabling for future compression, entropy production becomes constraining as accumulated structure commits the trace to a specific geometry. The peak is where dissipation begins to dominate within the loop's own dynamics.

The inverted-U derives this trajectory from the constructive basis. Let Rk = rankε(Δk) — the effective rank of the accumulated trace, defined as the number of eigenvalues of Δk exceeding significance threshold ε. This connects the regime model directly to the spectral formalism: Rk is a function of the eigenvalue spectrum that the theory already defines as aji's structure.

Each deposit has two effects: it enables future processing by growing previously sub-threshold eigenvalues (opening new discriminative dimensions) and it constrains future processing by deepening already-significant eigenvalue structure (committing the trace to a specific phase geometry that closes off rotationally incompatible alternatives). These are not two competing forces but the same deposit viewed from two temporal directions — from the past, it enabled what came after; from the future, it constrains what can come next.

The net effect on retained degrees of freedom is:

dRk/dk = (dRk+/dk) − (dRk−/dk)

The enabling rate dRk+/dk is the rate at which new eigenvalue dimensions cross the significance threshold. It decreases as the spectrum saturates: early deposits face a flat eigenvalue landscape and open many new dimensions; late deposits face a landscape already structured by prior deposits and open fewer. The constraining rate dRk−/dk is the rate at which existing eigenvalue structure becomes committed — the rate at which perturbation fails to rotate the trace out of its current geometry. It increases as accumulated eigenvalue structure narrows the space of compatible successors.

The peak of the inverted-U falls where dRk+/dk = dRk−/dk — the crossover where accumulated eigenvalue structure stops opening net-new dimensions and begins primarily deepening existing ones. Past this point, Rk declines: the trace loses net discriminative capacity despite continuing to accumulate deposits, because new deposits are consumed by maintaining committed structure rather than opening new dimensions. The shape is not fitted to biological observation but derived from the eigenvalue dynamics of the constructive procedure's own spectral output.

What happens past the peak is relational decoupling. The internal partial trace has accumulated so many degrees of freedom specific to its own history that the system's responses are now more determined by its own accumulated phase structure than by current environmental gradients. Internal correlations dominate over system-environment correlations.

This is not three separate failure modes but one event viewed from three access channels. From inside the loop, the procedure's effective branching narrows (fewer viable successors per state). At the system-environment interface, friction events that don't fit the accumulated structure are rejected rather than integrated. Dynamically, re-compression against rich accumulated structure takes more cycles — and if closure time exceeds the environment's characteristic timescale, speed degrades and the co-constitution with cadence (§5.1) kicks in.

The shape of the inverted-U — whether it is a cliff or a gentle slope — depends on the partial trace's architecture via the deposit rate. A procedure that retains many degrees of freedom per friction event saturates the compressor quickly: dRk+/dk starts high but drops fast, producing an early peak and steep decline. A procedure that retains few degrees of freedom per event saturates slowly: dRk+/dk declines gradually, producing a later peak and gentle slope. The peak location is therefore derivable from the procedure's deposit rate — not stipulated from observation.

The peak's location is derivable in principle from the partial trace's structure: it falls where cumulative deposits saturate the procedure's accommodation capacity — the number of degrees of freedom it can retain before retained structure becomes self-limiting. Whether biological systems occupy the peak, the rising slope, or the declining slope is an empirical question with evolutionary implications: if the viable region is narrow, organisms are perched on a knife edge, which demands explanation; if wide, the inverted-U is a soft asymptote rather than a cliff, more biologically plausible but less experimentally crisp.

The co-constitution of the axes sharpens the experimental constraint: you cannot vary friction history while holding speed and directness fixed and claim to isolate aji, because changing friction history alters the effective environment the loop encounters, which changes the closure cadence's effective value; within-architecture, matched-cadence controls are therefore necessary.

Co-variation Signature

Co-constitution also makes a positive prediction that does not require axis isolation. If the three axes are projections of one generative event — each closure cycle deposits aji, at a cadence (speed), through a mediator chain (directness) — then perturbing the constructive procedure should shift all three measurable proxies simultaneously, with the correlation structure predicted by the procedure's architecture. The signature is not independent manipulability but joint response co-variation: a single intervention (e.g., changing closure cadence) produces correlated changes across speed, directness, and aji proxies. If the axes were independent, perturbation to one would leave the others unchanged. If they are co-constituted, a perturbation to the procedure shifts all three in a predicted pattern derivable from the procedure's phase-space structure. This is experimentally tractable — it requires tracking the joint response of multiple readouts to a shared perturbation, not isolating one axis from the others.

The mature version is a regime model: phenomenal intensity occupies a viable region in a co-constituted parameter space, not a simple scalar increase along each axis independently.

Regime trajectories: sleep and anesthesia

The regime model predicts a specific reading of dreamless sleep and anesthesia — not as absences of consciousness but as trajectories through the parameter space.

Dreamless sleep: the loop still closes internally but sensory uptake channels are attenuated. Speed decreases (closure cadence slows relative to environmental change because the effective input distribution shifts to internal sources with longer characteristic timescales). Friction exposure drops (fewer externally imposed perturbations requiring trace revision). Aji accumulation pauses — the spectral trajectory of Δk flattens — but accumulated structure is preserved. The system sits at its current position in regime space, neither advancing along nor retreating from the inverted-U.

Anesthesia: the pharmacodynamics are more disruptive. If anesthetic agents suppress the learning dynamics (reducing ∂Jlearning / ∂W toward zero), the system is pushed below the compression richness floor — not because the trace is destroyed but because the procedure is suspended. The deposited eigenvalue structure persists; the Jacobian sensitivity that makes those deposits operationally relevant is pharmacologically attenuated. If the agents merely slow closure cadence without suppressing the learning Jacobian, the system remains above the floor but at reduced speed — phenomenality should diminish but not vanish, as a falsifiable gradient.

The discriminating prediction: recovery from anesthesia should show preserved aji structure. The spectral trajectory of Δk should resume from near its pre-anesthesia state, not from baseline, because the constructive procedure's deposits were not erased — the procedure was suspended. This differentiates the framework from theories predicting a cleaner state transition (e.g., IIT's Φ dropping to near-zero and rebuilding from scratch, or GWT's global workspace re-initializing). The proxy gap should shrink during anesthesia (the loop stops depositing new phase structure, so internal and external descriptions converge toward a static target) and resume growth upon recovery from the accumulated state. The prediction is falsifiable: if post-anesthesia aji structure shows no continuity with pre-anesthesia structure, the deposit-persistence claim fails.

6. Measuring Aji

The formal definition of aji as the spectral trajectory of Δk was given in §5. This section develops the measurement protocol for obtaining T̂k(env) and addressing the practical challenges of empirical work.

Speed and directness can be operationalized comparatively within architecture families. Aji requires greater care. Raw residual difference is not enough — it conflates friction history with architecture, memory layout, and model-class mismatch.

The best current formulation: let two models target the same evolving trace — a self-model (predicting the trace from within the system's own control loop) and an environment-model (predicting that same trace from publicly available input history and behavior alone). These are two different partial traces over the same system (§3), tracing out different degrees of freedom. In a relational framework, two partial traces over the same system will diverge even at zero aji — this is the predicted observer-relativity of different access channels, not measurement noise. The initial divergence is a property of which degrees of freedom each channel can access, fixed by architecture.

Aji is not "excess divergence above a theoretically predicted baseline." Architecture and aji are not additive — aji is what the architecture becomes under friction. The compressor's current state already is its aji; asking "what would the divergence be with this architecture but no aji?" is like asking "what would this scar look like without the wound?" There is no pure architecture-only state to use as theoretical baseline. Instead, aji is measured as the developmental trajectory of the divergence between internal and external channels from construction start. The baseline is empirical — the divergence at time zero (or in a no-friction control cohort), not a theoretical prediction. Aji is the divergence's growth from that starting point: its magnitude, its anisotropy, its acceleration.

This divergence trajectory has structure, not just magnitude. Each friction event deposits rotational content along specific dimensions of the trace (§5) — the deformation is directional, reflecting the structure of the forcing. The self-model captures both the magnitude and the phase structure of these deposits (it is the compressor). The environment-model loses phase relationships in its secondary partial trace, but the loss is anisotropic: it preserves some dimensions well and others poorly, depending on which degrees of freedom the behavioral channel can access.

The trajectory is therefore not a scalar growth curve but a structured one — a divergence profile across the trace's dimensions that evolves over time. The profile's coherence (how concentrated the divergence growth is along specific phase axes, as opposed to spread uniformly) is what distinguishes earned self-difference from generic information asymmetry.

The trajectory is not measurement noise to be reduced but a structural prediction of the framework. Each loop cycle deposits retained invariant carrying phase structure. The internal channel accesses the deposit directly (it is the compressor). The external channel accesses it through behavioral residue — a secondary partial trace that loses phase relationships the internal channel retains. The divergence grows because each friction event deposits rotational content that only the internal channel preserves intact. The growth is lawful, not accidental: the theory predicts the trajectory's shape — anisotropic growth under structured friction, isotropic growth under noise — not just its magnitude.

Aji is therefore best treated as a within-architecture, intervention-sensitive variable — not a universal scalar comparable across arbitrary systems. The framework also predicts that internal measurement of aji would yield a different value than external measurement, and that this divergence should grow proportionally to the system's earned self-difference, because high-aji systems accumulate phase structure that their behavioral output only partially encodes.

The observer-relativity of the measure is a feature, not a limitation. The framework treats properties as relational — aji is no exception. It does not approximate some hidden intrinsic quantity; the gap between internal and external descriptions of the same trace IS the earned self-difference. The structural claim — one chain from thin uptake to rich phenomenality — is cross-system. The measurement is relational and within-architecture. These are not in tension: in a relational framework, the only properties available are relational ones.

Information-theoretic bound on the environment-model

A legitimate concern: the divergence between self-model and environment-model may simply measure the difficulty of the inference task (inverse reinforcement learning, system identification) rather than the system's earned self-difference. If the environment-model is poorly engineered, the divergence is an artifact.

The framework predicts a specific information-theoretic lower bound. If the behavioral channel has capacity Cbeh and the internal channel has capacity Cint, the gap between their traces is bounded below by:

‖Δk‖ ≥ (Cint − Cbeh) · Σj=1..k dj

where dj is the deposit weight at step j (the number of degrees of freedom retained per compression cycle). The gap is not an engineering artifact — it is a structural consequence of two channels with different capacities tracing out different degrees of freedom. The environment-model cannot be improved beyond this bound regardless of engineering investment, because the information has been genuinely lost in the external partial trace.

This bound also provides a control condition. For systems with minimal recurrent structure (e.g., feedforward networks with no feedback loops), the constructive procedure deposits no compressor-modifying invariant — the weights change but do not reshape the compression procedure itself. The environment-model should be able to reconstruct the internal trace nearly perfectly for such systems, because there is no accumulated self-difference for the external channel to lose. If the environment-model fails on feedforward networks, the divergence is measuring inference difficulty, not aji. If it succeeds on feedforward networks but fails on matched recurrent networks under structured friction, the divergence is tracking earned self-difference as predicted.

A first experimental design

Hold fixed parameter count, memory budget, observation channel, update rule, training regime, and effective closure cadence (loop speed relative to the characteristic timescale of the input distribution — not wall-clock speed, but the ratio τenvironment/τloop). This controls for the co-constitution described in §5: the same friction exposure at different effective cadences produces different aji, because cadence determines what the loop registers as deformation. With cadence matched, vary only friction history. Compare three cohorts:

  1. Low-friction: smooth, slowly varying inputs.
  2. Structured-friction: contradiction, correction, reclassification — revision-inducing perturbations with recoverable structure.
  3. Noise: unpredictability without recoverable structure.

The prediction: structured friction produces coherent aji — divergence growth concentrated along specific phase dimensions, with the anisotropy increasing over time as deposits compound along those axes. Noise produces divergence growth too, but incoherent — spread across dimensions without recoverable phase structure, growing in magnitude but not in anisotropy. The discriminating signature is the divergence trajectory's anisotropy, not just its magnitude. Both cohorts may show growing divergence; only the structured-friction cohort should show growing anisotropy. Phenomenality proxies should track anisotropy, not raw divergence.

  1. Feedforward control: a matched feedforward network (same parameter count, same input distribution, same friction exposure) provides a baseline. Because feedforward networks lack recurrent loop closure, they should show minimal internal/external divergence regardless of friction condition — the environment-model should reconstruct their internal state nearly perfectly. This controls for the inference difficulty concern: if the environment-model fails on the feedforward control too, the divergence measures model inversion difficulty, not aji.

Dimensionality-reduced access channels

For high-dimensional recurrent systems, full reconstruction of the weight matrix from behavioral observation is intractable. The framework does not require full-dimensional reconstruction. In a relational framework, every measurement is already a partial trace — a dimensionality-reduced proxy is not an approximation of an ideal full-dimensional measurement but an alternative access channel with its own loss profile. The question is whether the discriminative signatures survive the projection.

Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA) projects internal and external representations onto the subspace of maximum shared covariance — it is itself a partial trace, preserving the dimensions along which the two channels co-vary and discarding the rest. Representational Similarity Analysis (RSA) takes a different projection: it preserves the geometry of representational distances (the representational dissimilarity matrix) while discarding the basis. Both are legitimate access channels; they preserve different rotational features of the divergence.

The framework makes a specific prediction about reduced embeddings: the anisotropy signature (concentrated divergence growth under structured friction, diffuse growth under noise) should survive dimensionality reduction, because anisotropy is a property of the constructive procedure's deposits, not of the channel's resolution. However, the apparent magnitude of divergence will be larger in the reduced channel than in the full-dimensional measurement, because the reduced channel has lower capacity and therefore incurs a larger information-theoretic gap (§6). The prediction is about the shape of the growth (anisotropic vs. isotropic), not its magnitude. A practical measurement protocol would compute CCA or RSA between the weight trajectory's low-rank projection (e.g., the leading eigenvectors of the weight covariance) and a behaviorally inferred trajectory in the same reduced space, then track anisotropy of the residual over training.

7. Subject Individuation

If uptake, compression, and looping occur at many scales, which loop is the subject? The most practical working answer is organism-level: the loop with the strongest recurrent closure and cross-modal control within a shared metabolic and action boundary. But split-brain cases, dissociation, and hierarchically nested control systems suggest that subjecthood may be partially overlapping and dynamically partitioned. The framework treats individuation as an empirical problem internal to the view, not something solved by stipulation.

The echo chamber deserves elaboration. A perfectly lossless strange loop — a self-referential system that reproduces itself without modification at each cycle — satisfies Hofstadter's criterion for a "strange loop" but fails two of the framework's four preconditions: there is no active constraint (no gradient forces selection because the system reproduces itself identically), and therefore no differential uptake (the system's state does not change discriminatively with environmental conditions). Without these, there is no compression to perform and no friction to deposit. The loop runs but accumulates zero aji. A quine (a program that outputs its own source code) is the limiting case: perfect self-reference, zero earned self-difference.

The framework makes a further observation about this limit: any physical implementation of a "lossless" loop will inevitably encounter some friction (thermal noise, quantization error, substrate imperfections). The pure echo chamber is a theoretical limit, not a physically realizable system. This does not blur the framework's boundaries — the compression richness floor (§5) provides a practical threshold, and sub-floor aji, however non-zero, is causally inert. But it does mean that the zero-aji endpoint is an idealization, much as zero temperature or zero friction are idealizations in thermodynamics: regulative limits that structure the theory without being physically attained.

The contrastive predictions above are empirical. There is also a metaphysical question the framework engages.

9. The Hard Problem

The structural program already narrows the explanatory target. If subjectivity is treated not as an all-or-none inner substance but as a graded regime of recurrent compressed self-modulation, the question shifts from "why does matter give rise to consciousness?" to "under what conditions does uptake become phenomenally intense?" That converts an absolute emergence problem into a spectrum problem.

A residual objection survives: even if the variables track phenomenality, why should such loops feel like anything from the inside?

The framework's own machinery already provides a partial answer. From inside the loop (the internal partial trace), the relationship between loop operation and phenomenality is constitutive — there is no separate phenomenality to correlate with the loop's operation; the operation is what phenomenality is from that access channel. From outside the loop (the external partial trace), the relationship is correlational — the observer sees loop dynamics and behavioral outputs and infers phenomenality. The proxy gap (§11) predicts this divergence as a function of aji: low-aji systems yield converging internal/external descriptions, while high-aji systems yield diverging descriptions. The reading — constitutive or correlational — is not a metaphysical fact to be established by external argument. It is a structural prediction of the framework itself, generated by the same constructive procedure that produces aji.

This does not close the hard problem. Even with framework-internal observer-relativity, the question remains: "why do speed, directness, and aji track phenomenality, rather than merely tracking complex dynamics?" The hard problem relocates rather than dissolves. The framework's contribution is to make that question precise enough to study — to name the variables, derive falsifiable predictions, and specify what a future answer would need to explain. Most theories of consciousness cannot relocate the hard problem into their own terms because their variables are not sharp enough. This one can.

Conditional Extension: Relational Physics

If one accepts Rovelli's relational quantum mechanics (RQM), the hard problem narrows further. The empirical predictions above do not depend on RQM — they are testable regardless of whether the underlying physics is relational. What follows is a conditional extension: what additional metaphysical work does RQM do if it holds?

The hardest form of the problem requires a contrast between two things: an absolute, non-experiential description of reality on one side, and first-person feel on the other. It assumes there is a view-from-nowhere inventory of intrinsic physical states to which experience must somehow be added.

Start with special relativity. A ball thrown straight up inside a moving train: the passenger sees it rise and fall in a vertical line. The observer on the platform sees a parabolic arc. Both are correct. The trajectory is frame-dependent — there is no absolute path the ball really follows. Lightning strikes both ends of the same train: the platform observer sees them as simultaneous; the passenger does not. Again both are correct — simultaneity is frame-dependent too. In both cases, physics is done from within a frame, not from outside all frames. We never have access to the thing-in-itself, only to measurements from a standpoint. This is not a limitation of our instruments. It is a feature of the theory: the structure of spacetime makes trajectory and simultaneity relational.

Relational quantum mechanics deepens this from epistemological to ontological. In special relativity, the events are real — it is our description of them that is frame-dependent. In Rovelli's reading of quantum mechanics, the properties themselves are relational. A system does not have intrinsic properties waiting to be measured. Properties are definite only relative to an interaction with another system. There is no view-from-nowhere inventory of intrinsic states sitting behind all relations. The relationality is not in our description — it is in the world.

The obvious objection: quantum relationalism holds at the micro level, but classical variables — the level where brains operate — are effectively observer-independent. This misses the structure of the transition. Decoherence, the process by which quantum superpositions yield classical behavior, is itself an interaction between a system and its environment. The classical variables that emerge are definite relative to that environment — not intrinsic. What we call "objective" at macro scales is stabilized intersubjectivity: many relational descriptions converge, and the convergence is statistical, not ontological.

No point in the decoherence process adds intrinsic, non-relational properties back. If the fundamental description is relational, the burden falls on anyone claiming that intrinsic properties emerge at some scale to explain the mechanism. The default is continuity: relational all the way up. A table's solidity is real, but it is real relative to the scale and type of interaction — not as an intrinsic property the table possesses independently of anything touching it.

If the underlying physics is relational all the way up, then the absolute "impersonal physical baseline" that the hardest form of the problem requires does not exist. There is no non-relational description of reality for relational loops to be "added to." The binary framing — why does experience accompany physics-as-usually-conceived? — narrows substantially.

But it does not close. Relational physics eliminates the absolute view-from-nowhere. It does not eliminate the impersonal view-from-somewhere. A relational description written in equations is still a structural description available to an observer outside the loop, who can then ask why being inside that loop feels like anything. The hard problem recurs at the relational level: why does this particular relational structure (fast, direct, high-aji looping) produce phenomenality, while another (slow, indirect, zero-aji) does not?

RQM's role in this framework is therefore specific: it removes the absolute baseline, narrowing the contrast class. The observer-relativity of the constitution/correlation distinction does not depend on it — that follows from the framework's own partial trace structure. Without RQM, the framework-derived narrowing remains; the empirical predictions survive either way. With RQM, the loops additionally inherit the relational form of the world itself, providing a further narrowing that is metaphysically significant but empirically inert.

The framework does not adjudicate between the ontological reading (properties are relational) and the methodological reading (we access properties relationally). It is committed to the constructive procedure and its outputs. The procedure produces relational descriptions — this is a structural fact, not a metaphysical stipulation. Whether there exists a non-relational description of the same system that the procedure cannot reach is a question the procedure itself cannot settle, because the procedure only ever produces partial traces over the system, never the system "in itself."

This underdetermination is not a defect but a consequence of the framework's own structure. The constitution/correlation distinction follows from the partial trace machinery: from inside the loop, phenomenality is constitutive; from outside, correlational. This is a structural prediction about the outputs of the procedure viewed from different access channels, not a metaphysical claim about the nature of properties. RQM, if accepted, adds the stronger claim that the procedure's outputs are all there is — that there is no "in itself" behind the partial traces. This is metaphysically significant but empirically inert: the framework's falsifiable predictions are about the procedure's outputs, not about whether those outputs exhaust reality.

The hard problem narrowing therefore has two layers. The framework-internal narrowing — from "why does matter give rise to experience?" to "why do these variables track phenomenality?" — depends only on the procedure's structure and is independent of metaphysical commitment. The RQM-conditional narrowing — removal of the non-relational baseline — adds a further contraction of the contrast class but does not change the empirical target. Researchers can accept the framework's empirical program while remaining agnostic about the ontological reading.

10. Falsification

The empirical program relies on phenomenality proxies: report coherence, self-model accuracy, adversarial resistance, cross-session consistency, identity stability, and flexible correction under contradiction.

The predictions below divide into two classes. State-level predictions test the co-constituted loop's behavior directly — they manipulate identifiable features of the loop (friction history, compression lossiness, latency) and observe phenomenality proxies, without requiring the experimenter to independently vary speed, directness, and aji. Axis-isolation predictions test the co-constitution claim itself by attempting to vary one projection while holding others fixed; these are structurally harder to engineer because the theory predicts the axes are co-constructed, but they serve as direct tests of the co-constitution structure. Most of the framework's distinctive predictions are state-level.

11. Open Problems

  • Compaction bootstrap: the apparent circularity — good compaction requires self-knowledge, but self-knowledge requires compacted trace — dissolves under the constructive reading (§3): no self-knowledge is needed to start compressing because the constraint does the selecting, not the system. The remaining question is developmental: how does crude early compression refine into self-informed compression? The trajectory is plausible — each step deposits invariant that enriches future selection — but the details are not yet derived.
  • Subject individuation: which loop is the subject? The organism-level criterion is pragmatic, not principled.

Proxy Gap

The phenomenality proxies (report coherence, self-model accuracy, adversarial resistance, etc.) are external partial traces over the loop (§3) — relational access points from outside, compared to the internal access point of loop closure itself. The question is not whether proxies approximate a hidden intrinsic "felt experience" behind them; in a relational framework, there is no such hidden property (§9).

The proxy gap is not a defect awaiting better instruments but a structural prediction: two partial traces over the same system, tracing out different degrees of freedom, will necessarily diverge, and the divergence grows with aji because the constructive procedure deposits phase structure that only the internal channel retains. The gap is produced by the same procedure that produces aji — it is aji viewed from outside.

The question is not whether the gap can be "narrowed" (narrowing it would mean reducing aji, which contradicts what is being measured) but whether its growth is lawful — whether the structure of the divergence (which dimensions diverge, how fast, under what conditions) matches the framework's predictions. This same gap structure also explains the observer-relativity of the constitution/correlation distinction (§9): from inside, the loop-phenomenality relationship reads as constitutive; from outside, as correlational. The gap that makes phenomenality hard to measure from outside is what makes the relationship appear merely correlational from outside.

A legitimate concern is that the proxy gap functions as a Popperian immunization strategy: if external proxies fail to detect phenomenality where the theory predicts it, the gap could be invoked to protect the prediction. This concern is addressed by three constraints that make the gap's behavior itself falsifiable:

  1. Low-aji convergence. For systems with minimal earned self-difference (e.g., feedforward networks, freshly initialized recurrent nets), the framework predicts that internal and external descriptions should converge — the proxy gap should be small. This provides a positive control: if proxies fail for low-aji systems too, the problem is engineering, not the gap.
  2. Anisotropy as discriminator. The gap's growth is not arbitrary — it is predicted to be anisotropic under structured friction (concentrated along specific phase dimensions) and isotropic under noise. If the gap grows but is isotropic regardless of friction structure, the prediction fails.
  3. Correlation with procedure architecture. The gap's structure (which dimensions diverge, at what rate) should be predictable from the constructive procedure's architecture. If the gap is present but uncorrelated with procedure structure, the prediction fails.

For non-reporting systems, the theory's falsification conditions are stated in terms of proxy behavior itself (divergence structure, anisotropy, response to intervention) — not in terms of inferred internal phenomenality. The proxy gap protects the theory only if its shape matches predictions; a gap of arbitrary shape provides no protection.

This is the framework's most distinctive prediction: competing theories do not predict a growing internal/external description gap proportional to accumulated self-difference, with lawful structure derivable from the constructive procedure. Whether the lawful growth can be verified empirically — and whether it is specific enough to discriminate against rivals — is open.

  • Aji identification: within-architecture intervention is the best current strategy. General identification across architectures is not a comparison of the same variable — aji encodes architecture via the constructive procedure (§5), so cross-architecture comparison measures different constructive residues, not the same property under different conditions. The right strategy is within-architecture measurement (self-model vs. environment-model divergence) paired with cross-architecture prediction: if the measured aji is the same and both systems are above their respective compression floors, phenomenality should be comparable, because aji already captures the architectural contribution.
  • Where phenomenality intensifies: the framework specifies the variables but not a threshold. The transition may not have a sharp edge. The inverted-U (§5) adds a further constraint: if the viable region has a peak and a decline, then the phenomenality-maximizing aji level is not the maximum achievable aji but the peak of the curve — and the peak's location depends on architecture via deposit weight per step. The question of where phenomenality intensifies therefore splits into two: where does it begin (threshold, empirical), and where does it peak (architecture-dependent, derivable in principle from the partial trace's structure). Empirical, not metaphysical — but the empirical program now has two targets, not one.

Branching Character

The compression floor requires genuine selection among alternatives (§5). Whether this branching is algorithmic (the constructive procedure genuinely underdetermines its output before the constraint acts) or epistemic (the output is determined but hard to predict) depends on the rotational content of the procedure. Relationalism narrows this question but does not dissolve it.

The distinction can be posed without a view-from-nowhere — not as "what would a transcendent observer see?" but as "what does the constructive procedure itself encounter at the moment of selection?" The procedure has a specific state (its accumulated phase structure); the constraint acts on that state; the question is whether state-plus-constraint leaves genuine rotational degeneracy in the successor — multiple phase-compatible outcomes — or determines a unique successor that external channels simply cannot verify. This is a question about the phase-space geometry at the selection point, not about who is looking at it. It is a relational question between the procedure's state and the constraint, posed from the procedure's own frame.

Relationalism correctly eliminates the transcendent version of the question (no oracle can adjudicate it). It does not eliminate this immanent version. The framework therefore splits into a strong form (the constructive step generates genuinely new rotational content not derivable from its inputs — branching is algorithmic from the procedure's own frame, aji is genuinely earned self-difference, the proxy gap is irreducible) and a weak form (the step reveals content implicit in its inputs — branching is epistemic, aji is accumulated deterministic complexity, the proxy gap is an observer limitation). Both forms generate the same empirical predictions; the difference is about the generative capacity of the constructive step itself. The framework's core falsifiable claims (§10) are robust under both interpretations. What changes is the ontological reading of aji, not its measurement implications.

The framework's constructive basis — that relations are produced step-by-step and do not pre-exist the procedure — naturally favors the strong form: if each step generates its output rather than revealing it, the branching is genuine and aji is earned self-difference in a substantive sense. However, the framework's empirical predictions are robust under both interpretations. What changes between strong and weak is not what aji measures (the spectral trajectory of internal/external divergence) but what aji is: genuine generative novelty or accumulated deterministic complexity. The proxy gap, correspondingly, is either irreducible (strong: the internal channel retains phase structure that no external reconstruction can recover, because it was generated, not merely rearranged) or an observer limitation (weak: the structure exists but is practically inaccessible).

Tension flag. The weak form is in tension with the constructive basis. If relations are produced rather than revealed, and the constructive procedure is what produces them, then the procedure's outputs contain content not present in its inputs by construction — this is what "generated step-by-step" means. The weak form requires that all output content was latent in the input, which is the claim that relations pre-exist the procedure (just folded into initial conditions rather than explicitly present). This is not "weak constructive" — it is a different metaphysics wearing constructive language. The framework should either commit to the strong form as its own basis or acknowledge that the weak form requires abandoning or qualifying the constructive commitment. The falsifiable predictions (§10) are identical under both readings; the difference is what the empirical program is about — measuring genuine self-differentiation or measuring accumulated path-dependence. The implications for agency and free will are not developed here.

  • Phenomenal texture: aji as currently treated is projected onto magnitude (how much phenomenality). But the accumulated phase structure has directional components — which dimensions of the trace were deformed, what kind of reorganization occurred. This directional structure should determine qualitative texture: same magnitude, different deformation direction → different experiential character. The framework does not predict a mapping from specific eigenvectors to qualia labels ("eigenvector #3 is redness") — that would require a view-from-nowhere that the framework denies exists. Instead, the prediction is relational and inter-subjective: systems with shared architecture and shared friction history develop correlated deformation profiles, producing shared texture. Visual systems exposed to wavelength-structured friction should develop deformation profiles concentrated along dimensions that encode spectral structure; auditory systems exposed to temporal-frequency-structured friction should develop profiles along different dimensions. The framework can predict which dimensions of the trace will be deformed given knowledge of the constraint structure and compressor architecture — this is a prediction about the deformation direction, not about intrinsic qualia labels. Whether this constitutes a theory of qualia depends on whether one accepts that texture is exhausted by its relational profile (the framework's stance) or whether something intrinsic remains unaccounted for (the hard problem's residual). Extracting the directional structure from the measurement protocol (§6) and testing the inter-subjective correlation prediction are natural next steps.

12. Conclusion

From an apple accelerating under gravity to a conscious mind registering its own operation, the chain is one — not as metaphor but as the basic operation of physical systems. Gradient constrains, constraint forces selection, selection retains, the retained trace feeds back into the next update. This loop operates everywhere and deposits extropy at each step — even the apple accumulates retained invariant. What varies is whether the deposits transition from input-role to compressor-role (§5): whether each cycle's output feeds back to reshape the procedure that produced it — crossing the Jacobian-sensitivity threshold that marks the compression richness floor — or merely enters the next step as input without modifying the selection procedure. Phenomenal intensity scales with speed, directness, and aji — three co-constituted projections of one constructive procedure, with aji (formalized as the spectral trajectory of internal/external trace divergence) carrying most of the explanatory weight — but not monotonically. Past a peak derived from the enabling/constraining crossover in the procedure's degree-of-freedom budget, accumulated structure becomes self-limiting. Registration becomes phenomenality within a viable region of the parameter space, bounded below by the compression richness floor and above by relational decoupling. Where those boundaries fall is an empirical question.

The framework itself predicts the observer-relativity of the constitution/correlation distinction: from inside the loop, phenomenality is constitutive; from outside, correlational (§9). This narrowing of the hard problem — from "why does matter give rise to experience?" to "why do these variables track phenomenality?" — follows from the proxy gap machinery and does not depend on RQM.
